# Source Card: Towards Deterministic Inference in SGLang and Reproducible RL Training

## Summary
This LMSYS/SGLang engineering blog (published 2025-09-22) describes adding a deterministic inference mode to SGLang by integrating Thinking Machines Lab's batch-invariant kernels, enabling reproducible outputs for RL training and evaluation. The team reports that deterministic mode collapses output variance to a single unique output across trials at an average 34.35% slowdown (versus TML's 61.5%), recoverable in part by CUDA Graphs (>=2.79x kernel speedup). Determinism is currently scoped to dense models (Qwen3-8B, LLaMa-3.1-8B) at tensor-parallel sizes TP1 and TP2; MoE and larger TP remain future work.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) SGLang integrates batch-invariant kernels (mean, log-softmax, matmul) from Thinking Machines Lab.
- (ev_002) Deterministic mode yields 1 unique output per subtest across trials vs 2-18 in normal mode.
- (ev_003) Average deterministic-mode slowdown is 34.35%, versus TML's reported 61.5%.
- (ev_004) CUDA Graphs give at least 2.79x speedup across attention kernels.
- (ev_005) Only TP1 and TP2 are deterministic; larger TP needs reduce-kernel modifications.
- (ev_006) Supports dense models (Qwen3-8B, LLaMa-3.1-8B); MoE not yet supported.
- (ev_007) Seeded Gumbel-noise hash enables reproducible sampling even at temperature > 0.

## Limitations
- Self-reported, first-party benchmarks not independently reproduced; the post was updated 2025-09-24 after initial publication.
- Determinism scope is narrow: dense-only, TP1/TP2-only, with MoE and larger tensor-parallel configurations explicitly out of scope at time of writing.
