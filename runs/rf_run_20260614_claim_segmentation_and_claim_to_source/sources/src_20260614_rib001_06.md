# Source Card: LongCite: Enabling LLMs to Generate Fine-grained Citations in Long-context QA

## Summary
LongCite (THU KEG, arXiv 2409.02897) targets faithfulness and verifiability in long-context QA by training LLMs to emit fine-grained, sentence-level citations in a single output. It introduces the LongBench-Cite benchmark, the CoF (Coarse-to-Fine) data-generation pipeline that builds the LongCite-45k SFT dataset, and trained LongCite-8B/9B models that surpass proprietary baselines (GPT-4o, Claude-3-Sonnet) on citation quality. Citation quality is measured via citation recall, precision, and their F1, scored by a GPT-4o judge with moderate human agreement.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Citation unit is fine-grained sentence-level: a citation references a sentence or sentence span in the source.
- (ev_002) LongCite-8B reaches citation F1 72.0 (R 62.0 / P 79.7); LongCite-9B reaches F1 69.2 (R 57.6 / P 78.1) on LongBench-Cite.
- (ev_003) Trained models beat proprietary baselines: GPT-4o F1 65.6 (R 46.7 / P 53.5), Claude-3-Sonnet F1 67.2 (R 52.0 / P 67.8).
- (ev_004) Citation recall scores each statement 1/0.5/0 for full/partial/no support by concatenated cited snippets.
- (ev_005) Citation precision marks a cited snippet relevant if it at least partially entails a key point of the statement.
- (ev_006) GPT-4o-as-judge agrees with humans at Cohen's kappa 0.593 (recall) and 0.655 (precision).
- (ev_007) The CoF pipeline auto-generates the LongCite-45k SFT dataset (44,600 instances) in four stages.
- (ev_008) Citation F1 = 2*P*R/(P+R) over citation precision and recall.

## Limitations
- Citation quality is assessed primarily by a GPT-4o-as-judge evaluator whose agreement with human annotators is only moderate (Cohen's kappa 0.593 recall, 0.655 precision); reported scores are benchmark-specific to LongBench-Cite and may not transfer directly to other domains.
