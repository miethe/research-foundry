# Source Card: RAGTruth: A Hallucination Corpus for Developing Trustworthy Retrieval-Augmented Language Models

## Summary
RAGTruth is a manually word-/span-level annotated corpus of ~18,000 naturally generated RAG responses (2,965 annotated instances across QA, data-to-text, and news summarization) built to measure and detect hallucinations under standard RAG frameworks. It introduces a four-way hallucination taxonomy (evident vs. subtle, conflict vs. baseless introduction) and benchmarks detection methods, showing that span-level detection is hard and that a fine-tuned small LLM can rival GPT-4 prompt-based detection.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Evident Conflict = direct contradiction/opposition to the retrieved source content.
- (ev_002) Subtle Conflict = divergence that alters the intended contextual meaning.
- (ev_003) Subtle Introduction of Baseless Information = inferred details/insights/sentiments beyond the source (vs. evident = unsubstantiated content).
- (ev_004) Corpus is ~18,000 RAG responses, manually annotated at case and word levels.
- (ev_005) 2,965 annotated instances: 989 QA, 1,033 data-to-text, 943 news summarization (17,790 responses).
- (ev_006) Evident hallucinations are detected more effectively than subtle ones.
- (ev_007) Span-level detection is hard: GPT-4-turbo 18.4% precision; fine-tuned Llama-2-13B 52.7% F1.
