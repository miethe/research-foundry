# Source Card: VeriScore: Evaluating the factuality of verifiable claims in long-form text generation

## Summary
VeriScore is an automatic long-form factuality metric (Song, Kim, Iyyer; arXiv, June 2024) that, unlike FActScore and SAFE, extracts only "verifiable claims" — a single event or state with necessary modifiers — and ignores opinions, hypotheticals, and examples. Verifiable-claim density per sentence varies sharply by genre (0.03 for fiction vs 2.31 for nonfiction), making low density a usable signal that a passage is non-factual. Human studies favored its one-step extraction over SAFE's revision pipeline 93% of the time, but only 55% of extracted claims verified as supported, and the authors document that complex, non-entity-centric claims requiring reasoning beyond search snippets are a hard failure boundary.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) VeriScore extracts only verifiable claims; FActScore/SAFE decompose everything including unverifiable content.
- (ev_002) Verifiable-claim density per sentence varies ~77x by genre (WritingPrompts 0.03 vs FreshBooks 2.31), so low density flags opinion/creative text.
- (ev_003) Only 55% of VeriScore-extracted claims were judged supported in the human verification study.
- (ev_004) Human raters preferred VeriScore extraction over SAFE 93% of the time (SAFE preferred only 26/360, 19 marginally); VeriScore drops SAFE's revision step.
- (ev_005) Open-domain verification of complex claims requires extensive reasoning over claim-part connections, not exact/related-term matching.
- (ev_006) The system cannot reliably decide if an unsupported complex claim is a hallucination — beyond what reasoning over search snippets can achieve — so such claims should route to review or an inference label.

## Limitations
- Self-reported evaluation by the metric's own authors; treat performance figures as proponent claims. The 55% support rate is measured on a 320-claim human-annotated sample (Section 3.3), not the entire corpus. This is the v1 arXiv preprint (June 2024); a later/published version may revise numbers.
