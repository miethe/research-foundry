# Source Card: Claimify: Extracting high-quality claims from language model outputs

## Summary
This Microsoft Research blog (March 19, 2025, by Dasha Metropolitansky) introduces Claimify, an LLM-based claim extraction system that decomposes question-answer text into independently verifiable factual claims through staged Selection, Disambiguation, and Decomposition. Its defining behavior is abstention: it extracts claims only when confident in the correct interpretation, labels irreducibly ambiguous sentences "Cannot be disambiguated," and requires every claim to be entailed by the source. The companion paper (accepted to ACL 2025) reports that 99% of extracted claims are entailed by their source sentence and that Claimify outperforms existing LLM-based methods.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Fact-checking LLM output commonly works by claim extraction into independently verifiable statements.
- (ev_002) Selection drops sentences lacking verifiable content ("No verifiable claims").
- (ev_003) Disambiguation labels irreducibly ambiguous sentences "Cannot be disambiguated" instead of forcing a claim.
- (ev_004) Decomposition produces standalone claims that preserve critical context.
- (ev_005) Claimify extracts claims only with high confidence in the correct interpretation (abstains otherwise).
- (ev_006) Each claim must be entailed (fully supported) by the source text.
- (ev_007) 99% of extracted claims are entailed by their source sentence.
- (ev_008) Accepted to ACL 2025; outperforms existing LLM-based claim extraction methods.
