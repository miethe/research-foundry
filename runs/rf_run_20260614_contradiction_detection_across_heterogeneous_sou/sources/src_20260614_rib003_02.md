# Source Card: ClaimPKG: Enhancing Claim Verification via Pseudo-Subgraph Generation with Lightweight Specialized LLM

## Summary
ClaimPKG is an end-to-end framework that integrates large-language-model reasoning with structured knowledge from knowledge graphs (KGs) for claim verification. It splits labor between a lightweight, specialized LLM that encodes a claim as pseudo-subgraphs to guide KG subgraph retrieval, and a general-purpose LLM that produces the final verdict and justification. The authors report state-of-the-art results on FactKG (9-12 accuracy points over strong baselines) plus zero-shot generalization to the HoVer and FEVEROUS multi-hop datasets; the paper was accepted to ACL 2025 Findings.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) A lightweight specialized LLM encodes the claim as pseudo-subgraphs to guide retrieval; a general LLM then issues the verdict and justification.
- (ev_002) On FactKG, ClaimPKG beats strong baselines by 9-12 accuracy points across multiple categories (state-of-the-art).
- (ev_003) Demonstrates zero-shot generalizability to unstructured datasets HoVer and FEVEROUS across multiple LLM backbones.
- (ev_004) Motivated by the observation that LLMs struggle with multi-step modular pipelines and KG reasoning without adaptation.
- (ev_005) Accepted to ACL 2025 Findings; submitted to arXiv 2025-05-28 (arXiv:2505.22552).

## Limitations
- Numerical gains (9-12 points) are read from the abstract only; specific per-category tables and HoVer/FEVEROUS magnitudes were not inspected in full text.
- The small-model-for-grounding / large-model-for-verdict cost framing is an inference relevant to KG claim flagging; the abstract does not quantify cost or latency directly.
