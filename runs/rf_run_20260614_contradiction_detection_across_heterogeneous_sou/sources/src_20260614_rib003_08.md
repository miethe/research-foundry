# Source Card: HALO: Half Life-Based Outdated Fact Filtering in Temporal Knowledge Graphs

## Summary
HALO is a low-cost framework for filtering outdated facts in temporal knowledge graphs (TKGs) without retraining the underlying reasoning model. It quantifies each fact's temporal validity with an exponential half-life decay, classifies facts as active vs inactive to assign non-uniform per-category half-lives derived from average update intervals, and drops facts whose temporal influence falls below a threshold theta. Across 5 baselines on three ICEWS datasets, the filter consistently improves reasoning accuracy.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) HALO filters outdated facts via half-life theory as a low-cost layer rather than full retraining.
- (ev_002) Validity decays as V(ti,tHF)=V0*e^(-lambda*(tc-ti)), with lambda = ln(2)/tHF.
- (ev_003) Half-life is the time at which a fact's validity halves.
- (ev_004) Facts are split into active vs inactive; frequently-updated active facts get shorter half-lives (non-uniform decay).
- (ev_005) Per-category half-lives are derived from the average update time interval.
- (ev_006) A fact is filtered as outdated when its temporal influence drops below threshold theta.
- (ev_007) RE-GCN on ICEWS14: 37.67 to 41.38 MRR (+3.71); 27.68 to 30.52 Hits@1 (+2.84).
- (ev_008) CyGNet on ICEWS14: 45.45 to 47.88 MRR (+2.43); 37.46 to 41.24 Hits@1 (+3.78); evaluated over 5 baselines on ICEWS14/18/05-15.
