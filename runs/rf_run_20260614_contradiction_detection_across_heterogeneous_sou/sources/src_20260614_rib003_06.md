# Source Card: Contradiction Detection in RAG Systems: Evaluating LLMs as Context Validators for Improved Information Consistency

## Summary
This arXiv preprint by Gokul, Tenneti, and Nakkiran (Amazon, 2025-03-31) frames contradiction detection as a pre-generation "context validator" gate over retrieved documents in RAG. It contributes a synthetic data generation framework that simulates three contradiction types (self-contradictory documents, contradicting pairs, and conditional contradictions across a triplet) and evaluates Claude 3 (Sonnet/Haiku) and Llama (8B/70B) on detection, type classification, and conflicting-document segmentation. The headline finding is that context validation remains hard even for SOTA LLMs, with results varying sharply by contradiction type and prompting strategy being architecture-dependent.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Context validation remains a challenging task even for SOTA LLMs, with performance varying significantly across contradiction types.
- (ev_002) Unlike prior work limited to 1-2 documents, this study evaluates conflict detection across many retrieved documents.
- (ev_003) A novel synthetic framework generates self-contradictory, pairwise, and conditional contradictions in retrieved sets.
- (ev_004) CoT prompting is architecture-dependent: +31%/+46% for Claude Sonnet/Haiku but -26% for Llama-70B on detection.
- (ev_005) On type detection, CoT hurt most models and smaller Llama-8B beat 70B by ~6%, implying understanding matters more than scale.
- (ev_006) Models show high precision but low recall - reliable when flagging, but they miss many real contradictions.
- (ev_007) The study targets context-context conflicts (among retrieved docs), not context-memory conflicts.

## Limitations
- Experiments are limited to Claude 3 (Sonnet/Haiku) and Llama (8B/70B); the authors explicitly note GPT-4 might follow a different pattern, so findings may not generalize across model families.
- The synthetic data generation framework lacks an automated quality-control mechanism and depends on human annotation, limiting scalability.
