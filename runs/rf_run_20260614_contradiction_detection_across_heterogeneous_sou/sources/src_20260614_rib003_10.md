# Source Card: Towards Effective Extraction and Evaluation of Factual Claims (Claimify)

## Summary
This ACL 2025 Main Conference paper from Microsoft Research introduces Claimify, a four-stage LLM pipeline (Sentence Splitting and Context Creation -> Selection -> Disambiguation -> Decomposition) for extracting atomic, decontextualized factual claims from long-form LLM output for fact-checking. Its distinguishing feature is explicit ambiguity handling: it identifies referential and structural ambiguity and extracts claims only when confidence in the correct interpretation is high, discarding sentences it cannot disambiguate. The paper also proposes a standardized evaluation framework with novel coverage and outcome-based decontextualization metrics, backed by a 6,490-sentence human-annotation study over the 396-answer BingCheck dataset.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Claimify is a four-stage LLM claim-extraction pipeline: Sentence Splitting and Context Creation, Selection, Disambiguation, and Decomposition.
- (ev_002) Claimify only extracts claims when confidence in the correct interpretation of the source text is high.
- (ev_003) When ambiguity is unresolvable, Claimify discards the entire sentence rather than emitting a possibly-wrong claim, directly reducing spurious claims.
- (ev_004) The Selection stage drops sentences with no verifiable content (e.g., opinion/speculation).
- (ev_005) The evaluation framework's two key innovations are granular coverage assessment and outcome-based decontextualization evaluation.
- (ev_006) A companion human-annotation study labeled 6,490 sentences over the 396-answer BingCheck (Microsoft Copilot) dataset.
- (ev_007) Discarding is rare: the largest share labeled "Cannot be disambiguated" was 5.4%, and only 0.8% of disambiguated sentences yielded no claims.

## Limitations
- Claim-quality results were validated via LLM and human review (one annotator is a paper author), not against an external gold-standard fact-check ledger; experiments used a single source dataset (BingCheck) and no metadata context.
