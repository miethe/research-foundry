# Source Card: MiniCheck: Efficient Fact-Checking of LLMs on Grounding Documents

## Summary
MiniCheck (Tang, Laban, Durrett; EMNLP 2024) is a sentence-level fact-checking approach for verifying whether LLM outputs are grounded in evidence documents. The authors train small models on GPT-4-synthesized factual-error data and report that their best 770M-parameter system (MiniCheck-FT5) reaches GPT-4 accuracy at roughly 400x lower cost. The paper also introduces LLM-AggreFact, a unified benchmark aggregating 10 human-annotated factual-consistency datasets, and releases multiple model checkpoints plus the C2D/D2C synthetic-data pipelines.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Small fact-checkers reportedly reach GPT-4-level performance at 400x lower cost.
- (ev_002) Best system MiniCheck-FT5 (770M params) reaches GPT-4 accuracy and beats comparable-size systems.
- (ev_003) LLM-AggreFact aggregates 10 existing human-annotated datasets across closed-book and grounded settings.
- (ev_004) Task is binary classification: score > threshold (default 0.5) -> label 1 (supported), else 0.
- (ev_005) Synthetic training data is generated via two methods, C2D (claim->document) and D2C (document->claim).
- (ev_006) ~30 min (with prefix caching) vs ~55 min (without) for the 29K-example test set on one NVIDIA A6000.
- (ev_007) Ships roberta-large, deberta-v3-large, flan-t5-large, and the SOTA Bespoke-MiniCheck-7B variants.

## Limitations
- The paper states LLM-AggreFact aggregates 10 datasets (not 11 as sometimes summarized); use the source figure.
- Cost (400x), accuracy-parity, and throughput claims are author-reported under their own evaluation setup.
- Throughput/variant details (ev_006, ev_007) are sourced from the project GitHub README, not the paper body.
