# Source Card: Knowledge Conflicts for LLMs: A Survey

## Summary
This EMNLP 2024 survey establishes a widely-cited taxonomy of knowledge conflicts in LLMs across three categories: context-memory (input vs. parametric knowledge), inter-context (contradictions among retrieved/source documents), and intra-memory (inconsistencies within the model's own parameters). For a contradiction_log across heterogeneous sources, the inter-context category is the directly relevant class. The survey separates analysis of model behavior under conflict from the capacity to detect contradictions, and synthesizes causes, behaviors, and mitigation strategies for each conflict type.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The survey's organizing taxonomy is three conflict categories: context-memory, inter-context, intra-memory.
- (ev_002) Context-memory conflict = contextual (input) knowledge vs. parametric knowledge in the model's parameters.
- (ev_003) Inter-context conflict = conflict among different pieces of contextual knowledge (contradictions among source documents).
- (ev_004) Intra-memory conflict = conflicting knowledge within the LLM's own parameters, from pre-training inconsistencies.
- (ev_005) The survey reports LLMs are highly receptive to external evidence even when it conflicts with parametric memory.
- (ev_006) Under inter-context conflict, LLMs can also show bias toward evidence aligning with parametric memory — behavior is not uniformly "over-trust external."

## Limitations
- Survey synthesizes prior literature; behavioral findings (e.g., ev_005, ev_006) are attributed to cited studies, not original experiments, and the two findings qualify each other (model preference depends on conflict type and setup).
