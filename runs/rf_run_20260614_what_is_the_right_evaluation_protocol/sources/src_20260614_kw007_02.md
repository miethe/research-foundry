# Source Card: A Survey and Benchmark of Automatic Surface Reconstruction from Point Clouds

## Summary
Sulzer et al.'s arXiv survey and benchmark (v3) defines a reproducible multi-metric evaluation protocol for surface reconstruction from point clouds in Section 5.5. Reconstruction quality is measured with three geometric metrics computed over 100,000 sampled points - volumetric IoU (occupancy overlap), symmetric Chamfer distance (mean bidirectional nearest-neighbor distance), and normal consistency (mean dot product of corresponding surface normals) - plus three topologic indices (component count, boundary edges, non-manifold edges). The protocol is grounded in explicit mathematical definitions rather than ad-hoc scoring.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Geometric evaluation uses three metrics: volumetric IoU, symmetric Chamfer distance, and normal consistency.
- (ev_002) Volumetric IoU samples 100,000 points in the union of bounding boxes and takes occupied-interior intersection-over-union.
- (ev_003) Chamfer distance samples 100,000 points per mesh and averages bidirectional nearest-neighbor Euclidean distances.
- (ev_004) Normal consistency averages scalar products of unit normals at nearest-neighbor correspondences, bidirectionally.
- (ev_005) Topologic metrics (components, boundary edges, non-manifold edges) complement geometric accuracy.

## Limitations
- This paper's protocol covers geometric metrics (IoU, CD, NC) and topologic metrics (component/boundary/non-manifold-edge counts). It does NOT define F-score, precision/recall at a tolerance tau, or Hausdorff distance; those metrics asserted in the upstream brief are not present in this source and must be cited from elsewhere.
