# Source Card: A Survey and Benchmark of Automatic Surface Reconstruction from Point Clouds

## Summary
This survey-and-benchmark paper (Sulzer, Marlet, Vallet, Landrieu; arXiv:2301.13656v4, Dec 2024) standardizes how to score a reconstructed 3D surface against a ground-truth surface. Its evaluation protocol (Sec. 5.5) reports three geometric metrics - volumetric IoU, symmetric Chamfer distance (CD), and normal consistency (NC) - each computed on densely sampled points (100,000) drawn from both meshes, plus topology indices. This mesh-vs-mesh, point-sampling comparison is directly applicable to scoring a crocheted-result point sampling against a target mesh (KW-007 evaluation protocol).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Three headline geometric metrics: volumetric IoU, symmetric Chamfer distance (CD), and normal consistency (NC).
- (ev_002) CD is computed from equal-size point sets sampled on the facets of both meshes, fixed at 100,000 points each.
- (ev_003) Symmetric CD = bidirectional mean squared nearest-neighbor distance between the two sampled point sets.
- (ev_004) IoU is approximated from 100,000 points sampled in the union of both volumes' bounding boxes.
- (ev_005) Evaluation goal: reconstructed surface as close as possible to ground truth in geometry and topology.
- (ev_006) Benchmark surfaces are single-component, closed, and manifold, enabling topology indices alongside geometric metrics.

## Limitations
- The source key_points supplied to this card (an F-score protocol with tau=0.005 for objects / tau=0.03 for scenes, 200k/1,500k sampled points, and precision/recall-within-tau definitions) were NOT found in this v4 PDF and could not be verified; that F-score-with-tau protocol belongs to a different paper lineage. Those claims were omitted per the no-invention rule. This card reports only what the actual source states: IoU, symmetric Chamfer distance, and normal consistency, all on 100,000 sampled points.
