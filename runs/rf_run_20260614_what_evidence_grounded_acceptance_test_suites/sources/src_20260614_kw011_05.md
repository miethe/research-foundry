# Source Card: Amigurumi Crochet Patterns from Geodesic Distances

## Summary
This Bridges 2024 proceedings paper (Ben Chen & Edelstein, Technion) gives an accessible overview of the AmiGo system for generating amigurumi crochet patterns from a 3D model. The core idea is to compute geodesic distance on the surface from a seed point and treat the equidistant curves (level sets) as the rows of a crochet pattern, sampled non-uniformly by surface curvature. It also describes when a closed model is realizable as a stuffed crocheted object and how multi-component equidistant curves trigger partitioning into separately crochetable parts.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Authorship and venue: Mirela Ben Chen and Michal Edelstein, Technion; Bridges 2024 proceedings, pp. 369-372.
- (ev_002) Method computes geodesic distance from a seed point and non-uniformly samples equidistant curves guided by curvature.
- (ev_003) Constant-height SC rows crocheted in the round make every stitch in a row equidistant to the seed, so geodesic distance best models crochet rows.
- (ev_004) Equidistant curves spaced by stitch height, derived from per-point geodesic distance to the seed, define the pattern rows.
- (ev_005) Multi-component / multi-cut equidistant-curve topology drives partitioning into crochetable parts with no-sew assembly.
- (ev_006) Realizability constraints: "crater" regions (neg. mean + pos. Gaussian curvature) are not realizable and are removed by inflation; saddle-like regions are handled by reduced-stitch sampling.
- (ev_007) This is an overview of the prior AmiGo paper (Symp. on Computational Fabrication).

## Limitations
- This is a short overview paper, not the full technical AmiGo publication; algorithmic detail is summarized rather than fully specified.
- The source text does NOT name a specific geodesic solver ("Heat Geodesics") nor describe segmentation via isolines of saddle points; those specifics in the upstream request could not be confirmed from this paper and were omitted. Segmentation here is described as driven by the topology (multiple components) of the equidistant curves.
