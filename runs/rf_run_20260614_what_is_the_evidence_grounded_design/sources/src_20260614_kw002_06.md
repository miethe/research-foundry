# Source Card: KnitWit B1 Workstream Report — Crochet IR / forward & inverse prior-art survey (ChatGPT Deep Research)

## Summary
This internal KnitWit Deep Research report surveys prior art for crochet pattern intermediate representations (IR) and for both the forward (pattern -> 3D visualization) and inverse (3D mesh -> pattern) problems. It synthesizes peer-reviewed work (AmiGo SCF'22, Digital Crochet Onward!'22, Stitch Meshes SCF'20, CrochetBench arXiv'25) and open prototypes to recommend adopting CrochetPARADE's public-domain DSL grammar as the Crochet IR and shipping a forward visualizer first. It explicitly flags licensing and integration risks (GPLv3 engine code, Yarnspirations-copyrighted corpus) and sequences the harder inverse generator as a later, experiment-gated payoff.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) CrochetPARADE engine is GPLv3 copyleft but its grammar is public domain, so the grammar is the adoptable IR candidate while the engine is an integration risk.
- (ev_002) CrochetBench compiled 6,085 real patterns into the CrochetPARADE DSL and validates outputs by compiling them, proving the DSL captures thousands of real patterns.
- (ev_003) CrochetBench tooling is permissively licensed (MIT, assumed) but patterns are (c) Yarnspirations — corpus is for validation/training, not redistribution.
- (ev_004) AmiGo (SCF'22) is the state-of-art inverse mesh->pattern pipeline using segmentation, curvature-driven inc/dec, and join-as-you-go (no public code; Matlab/C++).
- (ev_005) Digital Crochet (Onward!'22) is the graph IR with inc = multiple incoming / dec = multiple outgoing edges and a force-directed 3D layout.
- (ev_006) Guo et al. 2020 'current loop' edge type tracks the live loop, providing the basis for guaranteeing one continuous yarn path.
- (ev_007) Sequencing guidance: ship the forward CrochetPARADE-style visualizer first; add AmiGo-style inverse graph semantics later, de-risked by focused experiments.

## Limitations
- This is an AI-synthesized Deep Research survey, not a primary research paper; locator-level claims about the cited papers are second-hand and should be re-confirmed against the primary sources before any public-facing assertion.
- Several license/maturity claims are hedged in the source itself (e.g., CrochetPARADE GPLv3, CrochetBench "MIT (assumed from research)"); treat license facts as provisional.
- Inverse-method fidelity claims (e.g., AmiGo shape similarity) are reported qualitatively with no formal error metric (e.g., no stated Hausdorff distance).
