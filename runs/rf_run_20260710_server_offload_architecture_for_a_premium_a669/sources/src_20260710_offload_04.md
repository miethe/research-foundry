# Source Card: KnitWit design-spec.md (project seed)

## Summary
KnitWit's seed design spec (authored via ChatGPT) sets the product's core architecture stance: on-device AI is reserved for small parsing helpers while heavy generation is explicitly permitted to run cloud-based, and 3D is scoped to structural stitch-graph/mesh geometry rather than yarn-level physics. It defines a structured Crochet IR as the parse-to-render data contract, flags pattern IP/copyright as the top business risk (forcing designer-opt-in or user-private ingestion), and grounds the hard "3D->pattern" and "pattern->3D" capabilities in cited academic work (ACM/AmiGo and a 2025 EG force-directed paper).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) On-device AI is scoped to small parsing helpers; heavy generation is allowed to be cloud-based.
- (ev_002) 3D is structural geometry (stitch-graph/mesh approximation), not yarn-physics/photoreal fiber.
- (ev_003) A structured Crochet IR (rows[] of stitch ops with counts, plus constraints/assembly) is the parse->render contract.
- (ev_004) Pattern IP/copyright is the #1 business landmine; ingestion must be designer-opt-in or user-private.
- (ev_005) Cited ACM/AmiGo work grounds automatic amigurumi-instruction generation from a closed 3D model.
- (ev_006) Cited 2025 EG force-directed graph-layout paper grounds pattern->3D rendering.
- (ev_007) Parsing ambiguity is a named risk motivating structured/validator-gated extraction over raw parsing.

## Limitations
- This is a project seed/design spec authored via ChatGPT, not an independent or peer-reviewed authority; it is primary evidence for KnitWit's stated design stance and scope decisions, but its external citations (ACM, EG) should be verified against the cited papers directly.
