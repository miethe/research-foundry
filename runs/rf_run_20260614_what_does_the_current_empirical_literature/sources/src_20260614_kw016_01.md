# Source Card: CrochetBench: Can Vision-Language Models Move from Describing to Doing in Crochet Domain?

## Summary
CrochetBench is an arXiv benchmark (Li, Huang, Chawla; Notre Dame; Nov 2025) testing whether vision-language models can move from describing crochet to producing executable crochet programs in a domain-specific language. Across instruction selection, instruction generation, and step- and project-level DSL compilation, all models perform poorly in absolute terms, but the failure patterns diverge sharply between closed-source and open-source systems. Notably, open-source Qwen2-VL leads project-level compilation, which the authors read as evidence that long-range dependency and stateful modeling, not scale, drives progress on this task.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Instruction-selection accuracy sits near the 25% chance level for weaker VLMs (BLIP-2 0.2562, Gemma 3 0.2494); Qwen2-VL reaches 0.4196 and GPT-4o leads at 0.5811.
- (ev_002) Step-level DSL compilation: Claude has the highest valid rate at 52.1% over 119 examples.
- (ev_003) Project-level compilation: open-source Qwen2-VL leads at 21.0% valid, surpassing all closed-source systems.
- (ev_004) Open models hallucinate undefined stitches / malformed brackets; closed models emit syntactically valid but semantically inconsistent programs.
- (ev_005) Authors conclude progress comes from architecture/training capturing long-range dependencies and stateful operations, not scaling alone.
- (ev_006) Instruction-generation absolute scores are uniformly low (best: Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite BLEU 0.0482, ROUGE-L 0.2583, ChrF 30.20).

## Limitations
- arXiv preprint (v1), not peer-reviewed; results not independently replicated. Benchmark is crochet-domain-specific, so generalization to other structured-instruction domains is unverified. Exact example counts (119 step-level, 100 project-level) and per-model error ranges (non-adjacent errors 7.4%-51.6%) are drawn from the paper's own tables.
