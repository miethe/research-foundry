# Source Card: [Literature Review] CrochetBench: Can Vision-Language Models Move from Describing to Doing in Crochet Domain?

## Summary
This is a third-party literature-review summary of CrochetBench, a benchmark testing whether vision-language models can produce executable crochet patterns rather than merely describe images. It reports compilation success rates (CSR) at both single-step and full-pattern levels, an error taxonomy, and differing failure profiles between closed- and open-source models. The central finding is a sharp drop from step-level to project-level executability, pointing to long-range symbolic reasoning and 3D-aware procedural synthesis as the limiting bottleneck.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Step-level CSR tops out near half: Claude Sonnet 4 52.1%, Qwen2-VL 35.3%, DeepSeek-VL 32.8%.
- (ev_002) Project-level CSR for closed-source models (GPT-4o, Gemini, Claude) collapses to just 4-5%.
- (ev_003) Open-source Qwen2-VL surprisingly leads at the project level with 21.0% CSR.
- (ev_004) Errors fall into four families: syntax structure, stitch definition, labeling/reference, and structural/formatting.
- (ev_005) Closed-source models fail mostly on label/reference inconsistencies; open-source models on undefined stitches and syntax errors.
- (ev_006) A sharp decline from textual similarity to executable correctness exposes limits in long-range symbolic reasoning and 3D-aware procedural synthesis.
- (ev_007) The core gap is describing visual content vs. generating functionally correct, executable procedures with symbolic grammar, numerical accuracy, and topological coherence.
