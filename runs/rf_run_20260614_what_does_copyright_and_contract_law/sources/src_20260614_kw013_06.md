# Source Card: Terms of Use - Our House Rules | Etsy

## Summary
Etsy's published Terms of Use is a binding contract that constrains how anyone may interact with Etsy's platform and seller listings. It contractually bans crawling, scraping, spidering, and reverse engineering without express permission, separately bans disruptive "excessive requests," and routes any programmatic access through the sanctioned API Terms. It grants users only a limited, revocable license, gives Etsy (not third parties by default) a broad perpetual license over seller-posted content, and channels disputes into individual arbitration while preserving a court path for IP-infringement injunctions.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Anti-scraping clause forbids crawling, scraping, spidering, or reverse engineering the Services without express permission.
- (ev_002) Same clause directs API users to the separate Etsy API Terms of Use rather than scraping.
- (ev_003) Anti-disruption clause separately prohibits "excessive requests," making bulk automated downloading a distinct ToS violation.
- (ev_004) Users receive only a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to use the Services.
- (ev_005) Sellers grant Etsy (third parties only as Etsy authorizes in its sole discretion) a perpetual, royalty-free, sub-licensable license over their content.
- (ev_006) Disputes default to individual arbitration, but parties may seek injunctive relief in court for IP infringement/misuse.

## Limitations
- These are Etsy's contractual terms, not a statement of copyright or contract law itself; they establish platform-imposed obligations, not statutory rights.
- No public effective/last-updated date is rendered on the page; the arbitration provisions (Section 11) apply only to users in North and South America.
