# Source Card: Prohibited Items Policy (Our House Rules) - Effective Until August 11, 2026

## Summary
Etsy's Prohibited Items Policy is a first-party platform rule, incorporated by reference into Etsy's Terms of Use, that enumerates item categories banned or restricted on the marketplace. For the crochet/handmade-pattern context it establishes two load-bearing rules: (1) unauthorized replicas/copies and the patterns or designs that enable their creation are prohibited, and (2) sellers themselves carry the burden of following all applicable laws for what they list. The page also shows Etsy's "enabling artifact" doctrine - kits, instructions, patterns, and designs can be banned even where the finished item is the actual hazard - and routes copyright/IP infringement reports to a separate Intellectual Property Policy.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Unauthorized replicas or copies, and the patterns or designs that enable their creation, are prohibited on Etsy.
- (ev_002) Etsy defines counterfeit/unauthorized goods as imitations of an authentic good that use a brand's name, logo, or protected design without the owner's consent.
- (ev_003) Sellers are required to follow all applicable laws for the items they list (seller-borne compliance burden).
- (ev_004) The prohibition extends to kits, instructions, patterns, and designs enabling the creation of prohibited items - patterns can be banned independently of the finished good.
- (ev_005) Copyright/IP infringement reports are handled under a separate, dedicated Intellectual Property Policy, not this one.
- (ev_006) This policy version is effective until 2026-08-11, when a successor policy with an updated Section 2 takes effect.

## Limitations
- The dedicated Intellectual Property Policy at https://www.etsy.com/legal/ip - which would carry the notice-and-takedown mechanics, repeat-infringer discipline (shop closure), the "reselling downloaded designs/templates/patterns is usually illegal" guidance, and any June 2025 Creativity Standards change regarding scanned vintage patterns - is JavaScript/bot-gated and did not render through automated fetch on the access date.
