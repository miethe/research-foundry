# Source Card: Circular 33: Works Not Protected by Copyright

## Summary
U.S. Copyright Office Circular 33 (revised March 2021) is the official overview of subject matter that does not qualify for copyright protection. It establishes that ideas, procedures, processes, systems, and methods of operation are excluded under copyright law, that mere listings/simple directions (the recipe analogy) are uncopyrightable, and that blank forms designed only to record information lack protection. It also confirms that original literary expression, illustrations, photographs, and sufficiently creative selection/arrangement of content within instructional works can be protected.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Copyright law expressly excludes ideas, procedures, processes, systems, and methods of operation from protection.
- (ev_002) Registering a description of a system protects only the expression, never the underlying method or system.
- (ev_003) A mere listing of ingredients/contents or a simple set of directions is uncopyrightable.
- (ev_004) A recipe registration cannot cover the underlying process, the resulting dish, or any procedures/methods of operation.
- (ev_005) A recipe that creatively explains or depicts how/why to perform an activity may be copyrightable.
- (ev_006) Sufficiently creative selection/coordination/arrangement of specific content may be protected, but only that specific content.
- (ev_007) Blank forms designed merely to record information (not convey it) are uncopyrightable, as is their functional layout.

## Limitations
- This circular is an overview only; the authoritative sources are the Copyright Act (Title 17, U.S. Code) and the Compendium of U.S. Copyright Office Practices. It addresses U.S. law only.
