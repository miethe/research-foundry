# Source Card: Intellectual Property Policy - Our House Rules | Etsy

## Summary
Etsy's Intellectual Property Policy (a first-party legal document, part of Etsy's Terms of Use, published 2023-06-15 and effective 2023-07-24) describes how the marketplace handles alleged IP infringement. It runs a DMCA notice-and-takedown system through the Etsy Reporting Portal and a Designated Agent, removing material on policy-compliant reports while disclaiming any legal determination of infringement. It accepts DMCA counter notices for US-based copyright reports only, restores material 10 business days after a counter notice unless the owner files a court action or Copyright Claims Board (CCB) action, terminates repeat infringers, and reserves the right to reject bad-faith or fraudulent filings.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) DMCA notice-and-takedown runs through the Etsy Reporting Portal and a Designated Agent under the DMCA.
- (ev_002) Etsy disclaims making legal determinations of infringement; it removes material cited in policy-compliant reports.
- (ev_003) Counter notices are accepted for US-based copyright reports only; 10-business-day restoration window unless a court or CCB action is filed and Etsy is informed.
- (ev_004) Etsy terminates selling privileges of repeat/multiple infringers and refuses service to re-opened shops linked to terminated accounts.
- (ev_005) Etsy may share the reporting party's name and email with the affected member, and may reject false, fraudulent, incomplete, or bad-faith filings.
- (ev_006) The Copyright Claims Board (CCB) is explicitly named as a qualifying small-claims venue that can block restoration of removed material.

## Limitations
- This is Etsy's platform policy, not statutory law; it describes Etsy's own enforcement procedure (which mirrors but is not identical to the DMCA statute).
