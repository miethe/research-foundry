# Source Card: AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) and Generative AI Profile (NIST AI 600-1)

## Summary
NIST's AI Risk Management Framework (released January 26, 2023) and its Generative AI Profile, NIST.AI.600-1 (published July 26, 2024), establish a voluntary, cross-sectoral framework for managing AI risk. The Gen AI Profile enumerates 12 risks unique to or exacerbated by generative AI — including Data Privacy (framed around leakage), Information Integrity, and Value Chain/provenance — and organizes suggested actions around the four AI RMF Core functions (GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE). It directs organizations to document AI use cases, intended purpose, stakeholders, and data sources, making data-source governance and provenance first-class controls.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) AI RMF 1.0 released Jan 26, 2023; Gen AI Profile (NIST.AI.600-1) published Jul 26, 2024.
- (ev_002) The Gen AI Profile defines 12 risks unique to or exacerbated by generative AI.
- (ev_003) The Data Privacy risk is explicitly defined in terms of data leakage and unauthorized disclosure of sensitive/PII data.
- (ev_004) Suggested actions are organized around the four AI RMF Core functions: govern, map, measure, manage.
- (ev_005) Organizations are directed to document intended purposes, users, impacts, and data sources.
- (ev_006) A MANAGE action directs documenting training data sources to trace provenance of AI-generated content.

## Limitations
- The framework is explicitly voluntary; the "de-facto contractual pressure via federal procurement" interpretation in the source brief is editorial context and is not asserted verbatim in this NIST publication. Risk-count and core-function detail are drawn from the official NIST.AI.600-1 PDF linked from this landing page, not from the landing page body text itself.
