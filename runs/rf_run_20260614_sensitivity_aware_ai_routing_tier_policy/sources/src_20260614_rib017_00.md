# Source Card: FIPS PUB 199 — Standards for Security Categorization of Federal Information and Information Systems

## Summary
FIPS PUB 199 (NIST, February 2004) is the mandatory U.S. federal standard that defines three ordinal potential-impact levels — LOW, MODERATE, HIGH — for each of three security objectives (confidentiality, integrity, availability), expressed as the security-category triple SC = {(confidentiality, impact), (integrity, impact), (availability, impact)}. For an information system, each objective's value is set by the "high water mark" — the highest impact among the information types resident on the system. It applies to all federal information and systems except classified information and designated national security systems.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) LOW potential impact = a limited adverse effect on operations, assets, or individuals.
- (ev_002) MODERATE potential impact = a serious adverse effect (the middle ordinal level).
- (ev_003) HIGH potential impact = a severe or catastrophic adverse effect (top of LOW < MODERATE < HIGH).
- (ev_004) Security category SC is a triple over confidentiality/integrity/availability with values LOW/MODERATE/HIGH/NOT APPLICABLE.
- (ev_005) High-water-mark rule: a system's per-objective impact equals the highest value among its resident information types.
- (ev_006) Mandatory FIPS standard for federal information and systems, excluding classified information and national security systems.

## Limitations
- Scope is U.S. federal non-national-security systems; the LOW/MODERATE/HIGH semantics are normative within that regime and are not a general-purpose classification scheme. NOT APPLICABLE applies only to confidentiality for information types and cannot be assigned at the system level (low-water-mark floor of LOW).
