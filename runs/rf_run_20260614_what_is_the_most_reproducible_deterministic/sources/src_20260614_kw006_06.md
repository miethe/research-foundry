# Source Card: From Stitches to Digits and Back: Computational Crocheting of Branching Geometries

## Summary
This peer-reviewed 2022 paper (Eskişehir Technical University Journal of Science and Technology A, vol. 23, DOI 10.18038/estubtda.1169938) presents a parametric framework that converts digital NURBS geometries into text-based, step-by-step crochet patterns explicitly analogized to g-code in additive manufacturing. A 10-stitches-by-10-rows tension swatch captures crafter-specific gauge variables (yarn, hook, technique) so the same digital design yields dimensionally consistent physical parts across different makers. It extends to branching geometries by decomposition into multiple components, demonstrated by a 14-component structure crocheted by two architecture students (7 each).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Parametric model generates NURBS-surface crochet patterns calibrated by a 10x10 tension swatch.
- (ev_002) Output patterns are text-based representations explicitly likened to g-code in additive manufacturing.
- (ev_003) The method extends to branching geometries via multi-component decomposition crocheted by multiple crafters.
- (ev_004) Proof of concept: a 14-unique-component branching structure crocheted by two students (7 components each).
- (ev_005) Per-crafter tension swatches preserve dimensional accuracy across different users.
- (ev_006) Authors frame this as a collective, distributed crocheting platform bridging digital to physical.

## Limitations
- Proof-of-concept scale only: a single 14-component artifact made by two crafters; results may not generalize to large distributed pools.
- Scoped to branching geometries and NURBS surfaces, not presented as a general crochet-CAM standard.
