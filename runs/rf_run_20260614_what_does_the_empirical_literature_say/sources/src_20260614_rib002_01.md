# Source Card: Vectara Hallucination Leaderboard (HHEM-2.3)

## Summary
The Vectara Hallucination Leaderboard ranks large language models by how often they introduce false information when summarizing documents. Scores are computed with HHEM-2.3, Vectara's commercial hallucination evaluation model (open variant: HHEM-2.1-Open), over a dataset of more than 7,700 articles, last updated May 11, 2026. It measures intrinsic hallucination in a grounded summarization task (RAG-style), reporting a factual consistency rate (inverse of hallucination rate) and a separate answer rate.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The leaderboard is scored using HHEM-2.3, Vectara's commercial model, with HHEM-2.1-Open as the open variant.
- (ev_002) Computed over 7,700+ articles; last updated May 11, 2026.
- (ev_003) Measures document summarization (grounded synthesis), not single-turn QA.
- (ev_004) Primary metric is factual consistency rate (no hallucinations), the inverse of the hallucination rate.
- (ev_005) Answer Rate is a separate column capturing refusal-to-respond frequency.
- (ev_006) Lowest hallucination rates (May 2026): Antgroup Finix S1 32B 1.8%, OpenAI GPT-5.4-nano (2026-03-17) 3.1%, Google Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite 3.3%.
- (ev_007) Highest hallucination rates (May 2026): Mistral Ministral 3-3B (2512) 24.2%, Microsoft Phi-4-Mini-Instruct 23.5%, OpenAI o3-Pro 23.3%.

## Limitations
- Scored with Vectara's own HHEM-2.3 model, introducing potential vendor bias; this is a first-party leaderboard.
- Measures grounded summarization factual consistency, not general factual accuracy or single-turn QA performance.
- Figures are a May 11, 2026 snapshot; leaderboard contents change as models are added/updated.
- Multiple Ministral variants appear in the table (e.g., the older ministral-3b-2410 at 7.3%); the 24.2% figure refers specifically to ministral-3-3b-2512.
