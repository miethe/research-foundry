# Source Card: Introducing Citations on the Anthropic API

## Summary
Anthropic's first-party announcement of the Citations feature on the Anthropic API. Citations grounds Claude's responses in user-provided source documents by chunking them into sentences upfront and generating inline references at generation time, yielding sentence-level provenance. Anthropic reports internal recall-accuracy gains of up to 15% over custom implementations, and customer Endex reports source hallucinations dropping from 10% to 0%. The feature became generally available June 23, 2025 on Claude 3.5 Sonnet and 3.5 Haiku across the Anthropic API, Vertex AI, and (from June 30, 2025) Amazon Bedrock.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Citations returns references to the exact sentences and passages used to generate each response (sentence-level granularity).
- (ev_002) Documents are chunked into sentences upfront and passed to the model with the query, so citations are generated at generation time, not retrofit.
- (ev_003) Internal evaluations report up to 15% higher recall accuracy versus most custom citation implementations.
- (ev_004) Customer Endex reported source hallucinations and formatting issues dropping from 10% to 0%, plus a 20% increase in references per response.
- (ev_005) Generally available June 23, 2025 on Claude 3.5 Sonnet and 3.5 Haiku via the Anthropic API and Vertex AI; Amazon Bedrock support added June 30, 2025.
- (ev_006) Users are not charged for output tokens returning the quoted source text.

## Limitations
- Vendor (first-party) announcement with product/marketing framing; the internal-evaluation methodology behind the "up to 15%" recall figure is not disclosed, and the Endex 10%->0% figure is a single anecdotal customer report. Recency: published mid-2025; model/platform availability may have evolved since.
