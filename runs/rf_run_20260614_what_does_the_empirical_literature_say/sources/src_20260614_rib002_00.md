# Source Card: Long-form factuality in large language models

## Summary
This NeurIPS 2024 paper introduces SAFE (Search-Augmented Factuality Evaluator), an LLM-agent method that decomposes a long-form response into atomic facts and verifies each against Google Search results, plus the F1@K metric that balances factual precision against length-aware recall. Empirically SAFE matches crowdsourced human annotators 72% of the time, wins 76% of sampled disagreements, costs >20x less than humans, and is used to benchmark thirteen models across the Gemini, GPT, Claude, and PaLM-2 families on the GPT-4-generated LongFact benchmark (38 topics).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) SAFE decomposes responses into atomic facts and verifies each via multi-step Google Search reasoning — the decompose-then-verify claim-ledger pattern.
- (ev_002) F1@K balances precision (supported-fact ratio) against length-aware recall keyed to a user's preferred response length.
- (ev_003) SAFE agrees with crowdsourced humans 72% of the time over ~16k facts and wins 76% of 100 sampled disagreements.
- (ev_004) SAFE is more than 20x cheaper than human annotators.
- (ev_005) LongFact = thousands of GPT-4-generated prompts spanning 38 topics.
- (ev_006) Thirteen models across Gemini, GPT, Claude, and PaLM-2 benchmarked; larger models generally more factual.
