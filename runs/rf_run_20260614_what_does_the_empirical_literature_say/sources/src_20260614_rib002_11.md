# Source Card: Constitutional Classifiers: Defending against Universal Jailbreaks across Thousands of Hours of Red Teaming

## Summary
This Anthropic paper introduces Constitutional Classifiers — input/output safeguards trained on synthetic data generated from a natural-language constitution that specifies permitted versus restricted content. It reports that across over 3,000 estimated hours of red teaming no red teamer found a universal jailbreak matching an unguarded model's detail across most target queries, while deployment costs stayed modest (0.38% absolute refusal increase, 23.7% inference overhead). The work frames a content-policy gate against systematic jailbreaks, not a per-claim source-traceability mechanism.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Constitutional Classifiers are trained on synthetic data generated from a natural-language constitution defining permitted vs restricted content.
- (ev_002) Over 3,000+ estimated red-teaming hours yielded no universal jailbreak comparable to an unguarded model across most target queries.
- (ev_003) Deployment impact: 0.38% absolute increase in production-traffic refusals and 23.7% inference overhead.
- (ev_004) The threat model is universal jailbreaks enabling harmful multi-interaction processes (e.g., manufacturing illegal substances at scale).
- (ev_005) Enhanced classifiers showed robust defense against held-out domain-specific jailbreaks on automated evaluations.
- (ev_006) Submitted Jan 31, 2025 by Mrinank Sharma et al. (Anthropic).

## Limitations
- Red-teaming and deployment metrics are self-reported by the developing organization (Anthropic) and not independently replicated in this source.
- The robustness claim is empirical and bounded by the red-teaming effort expended; it is not a proof that no universal jailbreak exists.
- This is a content-policy safeguard, not a per-claim source-traceability mechanism.
