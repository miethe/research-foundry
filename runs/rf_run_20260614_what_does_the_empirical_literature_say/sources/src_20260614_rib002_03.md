# Source Card: How Much Do LLMs Hallucinate in Document Q&A Scenarios? (172-Billion-Token Study)

## Summary
A large-scale empirical study (JV Roig, Kamiwaza AI, arXiv, March 2026) measuring how often open-weight LLMs fabricate answers in document-grounded Q&A, using the RIKER deterministic ground-truth methodology across 35 models, three context lengths (32K/128K/200K), four temperatures, and three hardware platforms (172B tokens total). It establishes that every model fabricates at a non-trivial rate even under optimal conditions, that fabrication rises steeply with context length, and that model family predicts fabrication resistance better than model size.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Best model fabricates 1.19% at 32K; top-tier 5-7%; fabrication nearly triples at 128K and exceeds 10% for all models at 200K.
- (ev_002) The best single model, GLM 4.5, still fabricates 1.19% of the time at 32K.
- (ev_003) The median model fabricates roughly 25% of the time.
- (ev_004) At 128K, only 5 of 26 models keep fabrication under 10%.
- (ev_005) At 200K, no model is below 10%; best is Qwen3 Next 80B-A3B at 10.25%.
- (ev_006) Fabrication is probed with L11/L12 questions about content absent from the documents, so any specific answer is definitively fabricated.
- (ev_007) Higher temperature reduces fabrication for most models and slashes coherence-loss (infinite-loop) rates, which reach up to 48x higher at T=0.0 than T=1.0.

## Limitations
- Evaluates open-weight models only (no closed/proprietary frontier models); arXiv preprint with unverified peer-review status; the probe-based fabrication metric captures one specific failure mode (answering about absent entities) rather than all hallucination behavior.
