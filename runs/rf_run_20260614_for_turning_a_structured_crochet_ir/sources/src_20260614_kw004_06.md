# Source Card: AmiGo: Computational Design of Amigurumi Crochet Patterns

## Summary
AmiGo (Edelstein et al., ACM SCF '22) is a primary technical paper presenting an automated pipeline that turns a closed 3D triangle mesh plus a single user-chosen seed point into crochetable amigurumi patterns. Its core intermediate representation is a Crochet Graph G=(S, R u C) whose vertices are stitch tops/bases; rows are isolines of a geodesic distance field f(v)=d(v,s), and a column-ordering function g (solved by an optimization aligning columns with isoline tangents) fixes stitch orientation. The stitch vocabulary is limited to single crochet plus increase/decrease, join-as-you-go avoids sewing, increase/decrease rates between rows are bounded by a coupling relation enforced via Dynamic Time Warping, and curvature obstructions are resolved via Conformal Mean Curvature Flow and curvature-adaptive sampling.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) AmiGo generates crochet patterns from a closed triangle mesh plus a single seed point, producing a stuffed toy resembling the input geometry.
- (ev_002) The Crochet Graph G=(S, R u C) is the structured IR; vertices are stitch tops/bases, with separate row and column edges.
- (ev_003) Stitch vocabulary is limited to single crochet (sc) plus increase inc(x) and decrease dec(x) for local curvature.
- (ev_004) Instructions are crochetable, use only simple stitches, and use join-as-you-go so no sewing is required.
- (ev_005) Rows are isolines of a geodesic distance field f(v)=d(v,s) from the seed vertex.
- (ev_006) Column ordering g is solved by minimizing the integral of |<J grad f, grad g> - 1|^2, aligning columns with isoline tangents.
- (ev_007) A coupling relation (Definition 2.2) bounds inter-row stitch correspondence; minimal coupling between consecutive rows is found via Dynamic Time Warping.
- (ev_008) Curvature obstructions are handled by localized Conformal Mean Curvature Flow and curvature-adaptive sampling.
