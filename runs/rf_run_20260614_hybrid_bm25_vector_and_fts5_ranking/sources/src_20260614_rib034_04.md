# Source Card: Reciprocal Rank Fusion outperforms Condorcet and individual Rank Learning Methods

## Summary
This 2-page SIGIR 2009 paper by Cormack, Clarke, and Büttcher introduces Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF), an unsupervised method that combines document rankings from multiple IR systems by summing 1/(k + rank) across rankings, with the constant k fixed at 60. It is the canonical primary source for both the RRF formula and the now-standard k=60 default. The authors show that RRF consistently beats the best individual system, Condorcet Fuse, and CombMNZ on TREC runs (by ~4-5% MAP on average) and yields a LETOR 3 meta-learner that no prior reported result matches.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) RRF score formula: sum over rankings of 1/(k + rank(d)), each ranking a permutation on 1..|D|.
- (ev_002) k=60 fixed in a pilot and never re-tuned during validation.
- (ev_003) Pilot found k=60 near-optimal but the exact choice not critical.
- (ev_004) RRF beats any individual system and Condorcet Fuse (abstract claim).
- (ev_005) RRF outperforms Condorcet, CombMNZ, and the best system by 4-5% MAP on average.
- (ev_006) RRF is training-free and score-agnostic; no voting algorithm or global info needed.
- (ev_007) RRF meta-learner on LETOR 3 is the best known method per the authors.

## Limitations
- Short paper (2 pages); experiments are confined to TREC ad hoc/Robust tasks and the LETOR 3 dataset, and report MAP as the primary measure (P@k, R-precision, NDCG noted as "comparable" but not tabulated). The ACM DL canonical landing page is paywalled/Cloudflare-gated; full text here was verified against the authors' University of Waterloo open PDF copy.
