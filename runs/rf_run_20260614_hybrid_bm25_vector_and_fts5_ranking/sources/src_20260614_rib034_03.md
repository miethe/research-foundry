# Source Card: From BM25 to Corrective RAG: Benchmarking Retrieval Strategies for Text-and-Table Documents

## Summary
This arXiv preprint (T2-RAGBench, accepted at EACL 2026) empirically benchmarks retrieval strategies over 23,088 queries spanning 7,318 mixed text-and-table financial documents. It finds that sparse BM25 beats dense embeddings as a first-stage retriever, that hybrid fusion plus cross-encoder reranking is the dominant quality driver (Recall@5 up to 0.816), and that LLM-based query expansion (HyDE) actively harms numerical retrieval by hallucinating wrong figures.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Benchmark: 23,088 query-context-answer triples over 7,318 financial documents (~920 tokens each), from FinQA + ConvFinQA + TAT-DQA.
- (ev_002) BM25 (sparse) outperforms dense text-embedding-3-large on nearly all metrics (Recall@5 0.644 vs 0.587).
- (ev_003) Hybrid RRF + Cohere rerank reaches Recall@5 0.816, +17.4% over unreranked hybrid (0.695).
- (ev_004) Cross-encoder reranking is the largest single lift: MRR@3 0.433 → 0.605 (+39.7% relative).
- (ev_005) Contextual Retrieval (document enrichment) adds +2.8pp (dense) and +2.2pp (hybrid) Recall@5.
- (ev_006) HyDE underperforms vanilla dense retrieval on numerical queries (Recall@5 0.544 vs 0.587) due to hallucinated figures.
- (ev_007) Hybrid fusion's largest absolute gain is on table-heavy TAT-DQA: +8.1pp Recall@5 over BM25.

## Limitations
- Domain-specific to financial text-and-table QA; generalization to other domains is not established by this benchmark.
- Primary configurations use a single dense embedding model (text-embedding-3-large) and a single reranker (Cohere); results may shift with other models.
- The source's restated "+17.2pp MRR@3" framing was not found verbatim; the paper reports the MRR@3 lift as "+39.7% relative" (0.433 to 0.605), which is the figure recorded here.
