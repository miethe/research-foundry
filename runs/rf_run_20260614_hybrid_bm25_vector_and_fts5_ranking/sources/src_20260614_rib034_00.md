# Source Card: Introducing Contextual Retrieval

## Summary
Anthropic's engineering blog post introduces Contextual Retrieval, a RAG preprocessing technique that prepends a short LLM-generated context to each chunk before indexing for both embeddings (Contextual Embeddings) and lexical search (Contextual BM25). Using "1 minus recall@20" as its metric, the post reports stacked failure-rate reductions on a 5.7% baseline: 35% with Contextual Embeddings alone, 49% when combined with Contextual BM25, and 67% when a reranking step is added. Results are first-party and span codebases, fiction, ArXiv papers, and science papers.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The metric is 1 minus recall@20: the percentage of relevant documents not retrieved within the top 20 chunks.
- (ev_002) Contextual Embeddings alone: 5.7% to 3.7% failure rate (35% reduction).
- (ev_003) Contextual Embeddings + Contextual BM25: 5.7% to 2.9% (49% reduction).
- (ev_004) Adding reranking: 5.7% to 1.9% (67% total reduction).
- (ev_005) Contextual chunks prepend a short LLM-generated context (source filing, prior-quarter revenue) to each chunk before indexing.
- (ev_006) Evaluated across codebases, fiction, ArXiv papers, and science papers.

## Limitations
- Vendor-published, first-party benchmarks; results are not independently reproduced and exact corpus sizes are not disclosed in the post.
