# Source Card: Evaluating Chunking Strategies for Retrieval (Chroma Technical Report)

## Summary
This first-party Chroma technical report (Brandon Smith, Anton Troynikov, July 2024) benchmarks chunking strategies on a fixed retriever using token-level Recall, Precision, Precision-Omega, and IoU. It shows chunking choice can swing recall by up to 9%, with the LLMSemanticChunker (GPT-4o) achieving the highest recall (91.9%) and the authors' proposed embedding-aware ClusterSemanticChunker reaching comparable recall (91.3% at 400 tokens) with consistently strong results.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Chunking strategy has a significant impact on retrieval, with best strategies outperforming others by up to 9% recall.
- (ev_002) Chunkers are scored with token-level Recall, Precision, Precision-Omega, and IoU (IoU = |t_e ∩ t_r| / |t_e ∪ t_r|).
- (ev_003) Precision-Omega is the precision ceiling when all excerpt-containing chunks are retrieved.
- (ev_004) LLMSemanticChunker (GPT-4o) had the highest recall (91.9%) but low precision (3.9%).
- (ev_005) ClusterSemanticChunker is embedding-aware, maximizing within-chunk cosine similarity via dynamic programming.
- (ev_006) ClusterSemanticChunker at 400 tokens reached 91.3% recall.
- (ev_007) Authors conclude ClusterSemanticChunker produced consistently strong results.
- (ev_008) Recall plateaus then degrades with chunk size; too-large chunks dilute, too-small chunks lose context.

## Limitations
- First-party vendor report whose authors propose and benchmark their own ClusterSemanticChunker, so framing may favor it.
- Reported metrics carry large standard deviations (e.g. Recall 91.9 ± 26.5), indicating high per-query variance.
