# Source Card: Shallow Cross-Encoders for Low-Latency Retrieval

## Summary
This arXiv paper (Petrov, MacAvaney, Macdonald; March 2024) shows that under tight per-query latency budgets, shallow few-layer transformer cross-encoders outperform full-scale models because they can score more candidate documents in the same time window. The work establishes that reranking quality is latency-budget-dependent and that model depth should be chosen against the per-query millisecond limit, with shallow models further benefiting from gBCE training and remaining viable on CPU.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Under a 25ms/query limit on TREC DL 2019, TinyBERT-gBCE reaches NDCG@10 0.652 vs MonoBERT-Large 0.431 (+51%).
- (ev_002) Shallow few-layer cross-encoders beat full-scale models at fixed latency by scoring more documents per time budget.
- (ev_003) On CPU, shallow cross-encoder NDCG@10 drops only ~3% vs GPU at a 50ms latency budget.
- (ev_004) Shallow transformers benefit from the gBCE training scheme borrowed from recommendation tasks.
- (ev_005) Large-model cross-encoders (BERT/T5) are expensive and can only score a few documents per latency window.

## Limitations
- Extraction is abstract-level; full experimental setup and ablations were not read in detail.
- Reported numbers are specific to TREC DL passage ranking (DL 2019); generalization to other corpora/domains is not established by this card.
