# Source Card: Small Language Models for Agentic Systems: A Survey of Architectures, Capabilities, and Deployment Trade-offs

## Summary
This arXiv survey (Sharma & Mehta, Oct 2025) argues that small language models of 1-12B parameters (sometimes up to 20B) are sufficient and often superior for schema- and API-constrained agentic workloads such as tool use, function calling, and RAG. It attributes parity-or-better performance to guided decoding, strict JSON Schema outputs, and validator-first tool execution, claiming SLMs can match or surpass larger models at 10x-100x lower token cost with better latency and energy, while reserving LLM fallback for open-domain reasoning and long-horizon planning.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Defines SLMs as 1-12B params (up to 20B) and argues they are sufficient/superior for schema- and API-constrained accuracy.
- (ev_002) Claims guided decoding, JSON Schema outputs, and validator-first execution let SLMs match/surpass LLMs on tool use, function calling, and RAG at 10x-100x lower token cost.
- (ev_003) Synthesizes evidence across named SLMs (Phi-4-Mini, Qwen-2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B, Llama-3.2-1B/3B, Ministral-3B/8B, Apple 3B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill).
- (ev_004) Formalizes SLM-default/LLM-fallback systems with uncertainty-aware routing and proposes production metrics (CPS, schema validity rate, executable call rate, p50/p95 latency, energy per request).
- (ev_005) Recommends SLM-first design patterns while preserving LLM fallback for open-domain reasoning and some long-horizon planning.
- (ev_006) Authored by Raghav Sharma and Manan Mehta; submitted to arXiv October 4, 2025.

## Limitations
- This is a survey/synthesis preprint, not an original benchmark study; the 10x-100x cost-reduction and parity figures are aggregated claims over a range rather than a single measured result, and the paper itself delineates cases (open-domain reasoning, long-horizon planning) where SLMs are inadequate.
