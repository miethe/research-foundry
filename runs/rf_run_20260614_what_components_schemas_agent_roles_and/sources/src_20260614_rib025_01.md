# Source Card: Language agents achieve superhuman synthesis of scientific knowledge

## Summary
This arXiv preprint (Skarlinski et al., FutureHouse) introduces PaperQA2, a language-model agent optimized for factuality, and shows via a rigorous human-AI comparison that it matches or exceeds subject-matter-expert (PhD/postdoc) performance across information retrieval, summarization, and contradiction detection on real-world scientific literature. It also introduces LitQA2, a hard literature-research benchmark used to guide PaperQA2's design, and reports quantitative contradiction-detection results validated by human experts.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) PaperQA2 matches or exceeds subject-matter-expert performance on three realistic literature research tasks even with unrestricted human access to internet, tools, and time.
- (ev_002) PaperQA2's cited Wikipedia-style summaries are significantly more accurate than existing human-written Wikipedia articles.
- (ev_003) LitQA2 is introduced as a hard scientific-literature-research benchmark that guided PaperQA2's design.
- (ev_004) PaperQA2 finds 2.34 +/- 1.99 contradictions per biology paper, ~70% validated by human experts.
- (ev_005) Evaluation uses a rigorous human-AI comparison methodology over retrieval, summarization, and contradiction-detection tasks.
- (ev_006) Submitted to arXiv (cs.CL) on 2024-09-10, with a v2 revision on 2024-09-26.

## Limitations
- arXiv preprint authored by the developers of PaperQA2 (FutureHouse); claims are self-reported and were not independently peer-reviewed at the v2 revision captured here. Evidence here is drawn from the abstract and arXiv metadata, not the full results sections.
