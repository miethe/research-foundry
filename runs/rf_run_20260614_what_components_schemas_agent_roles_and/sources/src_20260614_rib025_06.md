# Source Card: Introducing deep research (OpenAI)

## Summary
OpenAI's February 2, 2025 product announcement introduces deep research, an agentic ChatGPT capability that conducts multi-step web research and synthesizes hundreds of sources into a cited report. It is powered by a version of the upcoming o3 model optimized for browsing and data analysis, trained end-to-end with reinforcement learning, and reports new-high benchmark results (26.6% on Humanity's Last Exam; state-of-the-art on GAIA). The page carries dated update banners tracking later availability and capability changes, including a February 10, 2026 update on MCP/app connectivity and trusted-site restriction.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Powered by a version of the upcoming o3 model optimized for web browsing and data analysis.
- (ev_002) Finds, analyzes, and synthesizes hundreds of online sources into a research-analyst-level report from one prompt.
- (ev_003) Trained end-to-end with RL on hard browsing/reasoning tasks; cites specific sentences or passages from sources.
- (ev_004) Scores a new high of 26.6% accuracy on Humanity's Last Exam (3,000+ questions, 100+ subjects).
- (ev_005) Reaches state of the art on GAIA: 74.29 (pass@1) / 78.66 (cons@64) on Level 1; 67.36 / 72.57 average.
- (ev_006) Runs 5 to 30 minutes per task and notifies the user on completion.
- (ev_007) Feb 10, 2026 update: connects to any MCP or app and can restrict searches to trusted/authenticated sites.
- (ev_008) Released Feb 2, 2025 (Pro first, up to 100 queries/mo); Plus access via the Feb 25, 2025 update.

## Limitations
- Benchmark figures (HLE 26.6%, GAIA SOTA) are vendor self-reported and not independently audited.
- The page is a marketing/announcement post; capability and availability claims reflect OpenAI's framing.
- Update banners are dated layers (Feb 2025 through Feb 2026); the original o3-based model and later lightweight (o4-mini) variant differ in capability.
