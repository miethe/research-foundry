# Source Card: Assisting in Writing Wikipedia-like Articles From Scratch with Large Language Models

## Summary
This NAACL 2024 paper introduces STORM (Synthesis of Topic Outlines through Retrieval and Multi-perspective Question Asking), a system for generating grounded, organized long-form articles from scratch at Wikipedia-comparable breadth and depth. STORM tackles the under-explored pre-writing stage by discovering diverse perspectives, simulating expert conversations grounded in trusted Internet sources, and curating an outline. The authors evaluate on a new FreshWiki dataset and with experienced Wikipedia editors, showing measurable gains in organization (+25% absolute) and coverage (+10%) over an outline-driven RAG baseline while surfacing residual challenges like source-bias transfer.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) STORM is a system for generating grounded, organized Wikipedia-like long-form articles from scratch.
- (ev_002) Pre-writing is modeled as perspective discovery, grounded expert-conversation simulation, and outline curation.
- (ev_003) Evaluation uses the newly curated FreshWiki dataset of recent high-quality Wikipedia articles plus outline assessments.
- (ev_004) STORM yields a 25% absolute increase in organized articles and a 10% gain in coverage breadth versus an outline-driven RAG baseline.
- (ev_005) Wikipedia-editor feedback identified challenges including source bias transfer and over-association of unrelated facts.
- (ev_006) Accepted to NAACL 2024 Main Conference; first submitted to arXiv on 22 Feb 2024.

## Limitations
- The 25% / 10% figures are framed against a single outline-driven retrieval-augmented baseline, and metadata/abstract were read from the arXiv abstract page rather than the full PDF; exact evaluation protocols and absolute scores are in the full paper, not transcribed here.
