# Source Card: FActScore: Fine-grained Atomic Evaluation of Factual Precision in Long Form Text Generation

## Summary
Min et al. (EMNLP 2023) introduce FActScore, a fine-grained factuality metric that decomposes a long-form generation into atomic facts (short single-information sentences) and reports the percentage supported by a specified reliable knowledge source. The paper argues binary per-document factuality judgments are inadequate because generations mix supported and unsupported content, and shows an automated retrieval-plus-LM estimator approximates human scores within a <2% error rate. It directly parallels Research Foundry's model of verifying each claim relative to a source rather than against global truth.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) FActScore = percentage of atomic facts supported by a reliable knowledge source.
- (ev_002) An atomic fact is a short sentence conveying one piece of information.
- (ev_003) Binary factuality judgments are inadequate because generations mix supported and unsupported content.
- (ev_004) Support is judged relative to a given knowledge source, not global truth (mirrors RF source-card-relative verification).
- (ev_005) Automated retrieval+LM estimator approximates human FActScore with a <2% error rate.
- (ev_006) ChatGPT scored 58% on biography generation; GPT-4/ChatGPT more factual than public models (Vicuna, Alpaca best public).
- (ev_007) Automated metric evaluated 6,500 generations from 13 LMs; would have cost ~$26K under human evaluation.

## Limitations
- Findings are anchored to 2023-era models (InstructGPT, ChatGPT, GPT-4, Vicuna, Alpaca) and to the people-biography task domain using Wikipedia as the knowledge source; absolute scores and model rankings may not generalize to later models or other domains.
