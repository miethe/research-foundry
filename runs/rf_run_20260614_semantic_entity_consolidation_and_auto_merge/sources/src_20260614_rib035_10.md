# Source Card: EnsembleLink: Accurate Record Linkage Without Training Data

## Summary
EnsembleLink (Dasanaike, arXiv 2026) is a zero-shot record-linkage method that matches records referring to the same entity across datasets without any labeled training data. It uses a three-stage retrieve-and-rerank pipeline: a union of dense embedding and sparse character-n-gram retrieval for high recall, a multilingual cross-encoder to rerank candidate pairs, and top-1 selection; an optional hierarchical-blocking extension fuzzy-matches a grouping variable (e.g. country) before searching within it. Running locally on small open-source models, it matches or exceeds probabilistic (fastLink) and supervised (fuzzylink, Ditto, DeepMatcher) baselines on political-science and bibliographic benchmarks.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) High accuracy with no training labels, leveraging pre-trained LMs' learned semantic relationships.
- (ev_002) Three-stage pipeline: dense+sparse union retrieval -> cross-encoder rerank -> top-1 selection.
- (ev_003) Pre-trained cross-encoder generalizes across domains; no per-task training or threshold tuning needed.
- (ev_004) Hierarchical blocking fuzzy-matches the blocking variable itself, unlike traditional exact-match blocking.
- (ev_005) Outperforms both fastLink and supervised fuzzylink on all four benchmark tasks with no labels.
- (ev_006) 89.0 pair-level F1 (tau=0.8) and 97.9% top-1 accuracy on DBLP-Scholar, approaching supervised Ditto/DeepMatcher.
- (ev_007) Task-specific fine-tuning gives no consistent gain over the zero-shot baseline.
- (ev_008) Runs locally on open-source models, deterministic, with accuracy stable at 0.98-0.99 across corpus sizes up to 64,263.

## Limitations
- Single-author arXiv preprint (v2, March 2026), not peer-reviewed.
- Baselines were re-implemented under the author's own standardized 40% test-split / top-1 methodology rather than reusing original published metrics, so cross-paper comparisons are not apples-to-apples.
- The source brief's framing (a Fellegi-Sunter ensemble of string-similarity comparators with named auto-match/review-queue/auto-reject confidence bands) does not match the actual paper; EnsembleLink is a dense+sparse retrieval plus cross-encoder rerank system, and the Fellegi-Sunter framework appears only as the baseline method fastLink implements.
- Assumes a reference corpus of moderate size (thousands to hundreds of thousands of records); very large corpora may need additional filtering and very small corpora may not benefit.
