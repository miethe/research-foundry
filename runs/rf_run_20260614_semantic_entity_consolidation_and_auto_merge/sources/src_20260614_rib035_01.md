# Source Card: The Fellegi-Sunter Model

## Summary
This Splink documentation page explains the Fellegi-Sunter probabilistic record-linkage model in terms of three parameters: lambda (the prior probability any two records match), the m probability (P(observation | match), a proxy for data quality), and the u probability (P(observation | non-match), a proxy for coincidental agreement). It shows how per-feature Bayes factors combine into an additive match weight under a conditional-independence assumption, and how that weight converts to a final match probability via a logistic-style formula, illustrated with a worked example where a match weight of 9.48 yields a probability of about 0.999.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Three core parameters define the model: lambda, m, and u.
- (ev_002) Lambda is the prior match probability, dependent on record count, duplicate prevalence, and dataset overlap.
- (ev_003) m = P(observation | match), a measure of data quality (e.g. DOB matches ~98% for true matches).
- (ev_004) u = P(observation | non-match), a measure of coincidence (e.g. surname agrees ~0.5% by chance).
- (ev_005) Total match weight = log2(lambda/(1-lambda)) + sum of log2(m_i/u_i); weights are additive under conditional independence.
- (ev_006) Worked waterfall example sums prior and feature weights to a total of 9.48.
- (ev_007) Match probability = 2^M / (1 + 2^M); a weight of 9.48 yields ~0.999.
- (ev_008) The weight-to-probability mapping is non-linear with diminishing returns (weight 0 -> 0.5, weight 7 -> 0.99).

## Limitations
- The m, u, and lambda values shown are illustrative pedagogical examples, not empirically measured parameters.
- Additive combination of match weights relies on a conditional-independence assumption across comparison features, which may not hold for correlated fields.
