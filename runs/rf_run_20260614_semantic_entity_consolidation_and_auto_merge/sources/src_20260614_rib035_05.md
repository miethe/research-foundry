# Source Card: Match, Compare, or Select? An Investigation of Large Language Models for Entity Matching

## Summary
This arXiv paper (Wang et al., 2024; v3 2024-12-12) investigates three strategies for LLM-based entity matching — matching, comparing, and selecting — and finds that incorporating record interactions via the selecting strategy outperforms conventional independent pairwise (binary) matching. It proposes ComEM, a compound framework that first filters candidates with a medium-sized LLM (matching/comparing) then identifies matches among the top-k with a more powerful LLM (selecting), achieving both higher F1 and lower cost across 8 ER datasets and 10 LLMs.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) ComEM composes multiple strategies and LLMs to gain effectiveness and efficiency.
- (ev_002) Binary pairwise matching ignores global record-relationship consistency, motivating the work.
- (ev_003) Evaluation spans 8 ER datasets and 10 LLMs; selecting strategy is superior.
- (ev_004) ComEM is a two-stage filter (medium LLM) then identify top-k (powerful LLM) pipeline.
- (ev_005) ComEM F1: Abt-Buy 87.62, Amazon-Google 69.63, DBLP-ACM 90.85, DBLP-Scholar 84.68, Walmart-Amazon 86.37.
- (ev_006) Supervised baselines: Ditto 80.69 mean F1, HierGAT 83.34 mean F1.
- (ev_007) ComEM gains 2~18% F1 while spending less, improving precision without losing recall.
- (ev_008) 10 LLMs span GPT-4o-mini, GPT-3.5-Turbo, Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2-7B, Mistral-7B, Mixtral-8x7B, Command-R-35B, Flan-T5-XXL, Flan-UL2, Solar-10.7B.

## Limitations
