# Source Card: A Survey on the Feedback Mechanism of LLM-based AI Agents

## Summary
This IJCAI-25 Survey Track paper (pp. 10582-10592) presents what the authors describe as the first comprehensive survey of feedback mechanisms for LLM-based AI agents. It introduces a unified five-module agent framework (perception, planning, feedback, memory, action) and a structured taxonomy of feedback into four categories (internal, external, multi-agent, human), then reviews evaluation protocols, benchmarks, and a comparative experiment across HotpotQA, ALFWorld, and WebShop. Its central motivation is that LLM agents still struggle to learn efficiently from feedback and iteratively optimize their strategies.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Survey frames the core challenge: LLM agents cannot yet efficiently learn from feedback and iteratively optimize strategies.
- (ev_002) Four-category taxonomy: internal, external, multi-agent, and human feedback.
- (ev_003) Authors claim it is the first comprehensive survey covering methodologies, evaluation protocols, and benchmarks.
- (ev_004) Proposed unified agent framework has five modules: perception, planning, feedback, memory, action.
- (ev_005) Evaluation split into outcome-based vs. process-based, with outcome-based dominant and tied to task success rates.
- (ev_006) Human feedback subdivides into instructional, corrective, and preference-based forms.
- (ev_007) Comparison on HotpotQA/ALFWorld/WebShop shows iterative-learning methods (Reflexion, ExpeL, AutoGuide) are more stable than non-learning methods (CoT, Act, ReAct).

## Limitations
- Survey/review, not original empirical research; the §5 comparative results are partly borrowed from prior work (ExpeL, AutoGuide), so they reflect aggregated literature rather than a fresh controlled study.
- The "first comprehensive survey" framing is an authorial novelty claim, not independently verified.
