# Source Card: AstaBench: Rigorous Benchmarking of AI Agents with a Scientific Research Suite

## Summary
AstaBench (ICLR 2026, AllenAI/Asta team) is a benchmark suite of 2400+ problems spanning the full scientific discovery pipeline, built to evaluate AI research agents under controlled, reproducible conditions with production-grade search tools. It explicitly accounts for confounders like model cost and tool access, and its extensive evaluation of 57 agents across 22 agent classes concludes that AI remains far from solving science research assistance. For RF adapter selection it offers a neutral, cost-aware cross-system frontier including literature-understanding tasks comparable to those used by systems like PaperQA2.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) AstaBench is published at ICLR 2026 and was submitted to arXiv on 2025-10-24.
- (ev_002) Headline finding: across 57 agents / 22 agent classes, AI remains far from solving science research assistance.
- (ev_003) The suite comprises 2400+ problems across the discovery pipeline, many from real Asta-agent user requests.
- (ev_004) The benchmark is explicitly cost-aware, faulting prior work for ignoring model cost and tool access.
- (ev_005) It provides a reproducible research environment with production-grade search tools, nine Asta agent classes, and baselines.
- (ev_006) It spans general-purpose deep-research systems through specialized science agents (AI Scientist, AIGS).

## Limitations
- Specific cost-per-query and per-task accuracy figures (e.g., LitQA2-FullText numbers cited in the source brief) reside in the paper body and tables and were not verified from the arXiv abstract page; treat those exact figures as unconfirmed until the full PDF is read.
