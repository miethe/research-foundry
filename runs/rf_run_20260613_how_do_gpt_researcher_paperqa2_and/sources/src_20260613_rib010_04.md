# Source Card: Language agents achieve superhuman synthesis of scientific knowledge

## Summary
This arXiv preprint (PaperQA2; Skarlinski et al., Sept 2024) presents a rigorous human-AI comparison methodology evaluating a factuality-optimized language model agent on real-world literature research tasks spanning information retrieval, summarization, and contradiction detection. It reports that PaperQA2 matches or exceeds subject-matter-expert performance under unrestricted human conditions, writes citation-backed summaries more accurate than human Wikipedia articles, and surfaces human-validated contradictions in biology literature. The work also introduces the LitQA2 benchmark.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) PaperQA2 is a factuality-optimized agent that matches or exceeds expert performance on three realistic literature research tasks.
- (ev_002) Human experts in the comparison had full, unrestricted access to internet, search tools, and time.
- (ev_003) PaperQA2 produces cited summaries significantly more accurate than human-written Wikipedia articles.
- (ev_004) LitQA2, a hard literature-research benchmark, guided PaperQA2's design and it exceeded human performance.
- (ev_005) PaperQA2 finds 2.34 +/- 1.99 contradictions per biology paper, with 70% human-validated.
- (ev_006) The authors conclude language agents can now exceed domain experts on meaningful scientific-literature tasks.

## Limitations
- Source is a preprint (arXiv, Sept 2024); evidence is drawn from the abstract only, not the full methods/results, so detailed experimental conditions, sample sizes, and which underlying models were used are not captured here.
