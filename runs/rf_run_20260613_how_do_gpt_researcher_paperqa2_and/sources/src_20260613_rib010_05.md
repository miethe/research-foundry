# Source Card: PaperQA2: Superhuman scientific literature search

## Summary
This FutureHouse announcement (Sept 11, 2024, by Sam Rodriques) introduces PaperQA2, an agent for retrieving and summarizing scientific literature that the org claims is the first to reach "superhuman" performance, outscoring PhD- and postdoc-level biology researchers on the LitQA2 eval (part of LAB-Bench). It also describes two agents built on PaperQA2: WikiCrow, which produces gene summaries judged more accurate than human Wikipedia articles, and ContraCrow, which detects an average of 2.34 contradicted statements per biology paper. Detailed quantitative benchmark figures (e.g., LitQA2 precision/accuracy, the 248-question count) reside in the linked arXiv paper rather than on this announcement page.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) PaperQA2 announced as first AI agent with superhuman performance on scientific literature search tasks.
- (ev_002) PaperQA2 beats PhD/postdoc biology researchers on LitQA2 (part of LAB-Bench) at retrieving literature information.
- (ev_003) WikiCrow (built on PaperQA2) summaries judged more accurate than human-curated Wikipedia articles by blinded experts.
- (ev_004) Older PaperQA generated articles for all 20,000 human genes from 1M papers, but was then less accurate than Wikipedia.
- (ev_005) ContraCrow (built on PaperQA2) finds 2.34 contradicted statements per paper on average in a random biology-paper subset.

## Limitations
- First-party promotional announcement from the developing org (FutureHouse); accuracy claims are vendor self-report against their own LitQA2/LAB-Bench eval.
- The headline quantitative LitQA2 metrics in the run's key_points (85.2% precision, 66.0% accuracy, 248 questions, ~70% human-validated contradictions) are NOT stated verbatim on this announcement page; they belong to the linked arXiv paper (arxiv.org/abs/2409.13740) and should be cited from that source, not this card.
