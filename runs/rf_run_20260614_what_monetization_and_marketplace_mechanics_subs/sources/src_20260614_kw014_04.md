# Source Card: App Store Small Business Program - Apple Developer

## Summary
Apple's official App Store Small Business Program page sets out a reduced 15% App Store commission rate (vs. the standard 30%) on paid apps and In-App Purchases for eligible small developers. Eligibility is gated on total proceeds of no more than 1 million USD during the 12 fiscal months in the prior calendar year (and not exceeding that in the current year), measured across the developer's Associated Developer Accounts. New developers also qualify, and crossing the 1 million USD threshold in the current year reverts future sales to the standard commission rate.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The program offers a reduced 15% commission rate on paid apps and In-App Purchases.
- (ev_002) Existing developers under 1 million USD proceeds in the prior calendar year, plus new developers, qualify.
- (ev_003) Eligibility threshold is no more than 1 million USD in total proceeds over the 12 fiscal months in the previous calendar year (and in the current year), across Associated Developer Accounts.
- (ev_004) Exceeding the 1 million USD threshold in the current calendar year reverts future sales to the standard commission rate.

## Limitations
- The standard (30%) commission rate is not explicitly stated on this page; the 30% figure must be sourced separately.
- The page lists no specific program or enrollment dates; the site copyright reads 2026 while the program itself dates to 2020-2021.
