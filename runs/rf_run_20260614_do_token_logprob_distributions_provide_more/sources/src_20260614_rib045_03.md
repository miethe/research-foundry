# Source Card: Selective Classification for Deep Neural Networks

## Summary
This NeurIPS 2017 paper by Geifman and El-Yaniv introduces selective classification (the "reject option") for deep neural networks, formalizing a selective classifier as a predictor f paired with a selection function g that abstains on uncertain inputs. It defines selective risk (error over accepted instances) and coverage (fraction accepted), and presents the SGR (Selection with Guaranteed Risk) algorithm that lets a user fix a target risk r* and guarantees it with probability at least 1-delta. Empirically it demonstrates the coverage-versus-risk trade, e.g. ~2% top-5 ImageNet error guaranteed at ~60% coverage.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) A selective classifier is the pair (f, g): a predictor plus a selection/rejection function.
- (ev_002) Coverage is the probability mass of the non-rejected (accepted) region of inputs.
- (ev_003) Selective risk is expected loss over accepted instances, normalized by coverage.
- (ev_004) SGR guarantees the selective risk stays at or below a user-set target r* with probability at least 1-delta.
- (ev_005) The risk-coverage (RC) curve plots risk as a function of coverage and captures the full performance profile.
- (ev_006) Reported result: ~2% top-5 ImageNet error guaranteed with probability 99.9% at almost 60% test coverage.

## Limitations
- The "area under the RC curve as the summary metric" framing could not be confirmed as verbatim in this paper, so it is not asserted as a quoted claim here.
- Empirical numbers reflect 2017-era architectures; absolute error/coverage figures are not current state of the art and should be cited as historical/foundational.
