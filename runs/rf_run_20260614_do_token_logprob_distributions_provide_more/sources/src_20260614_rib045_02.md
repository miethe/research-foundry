# Source Card: On Calibration of Modern Neural Networks

## Summary
Guo, Pleiss, Sun, and Weinberger (ICML 2017, PMLR v70) establish the canonical definitions used across the calibration literature: reliability diagrams, Expected Calibration Error (ECE) over M equally-spaced bins (standard M=15), and temperature scaling. They show modern deep classifiers (e.g., ResNets) are systematically overconfident and that capacity factors (depth, width, weight decay, BatchNorm) worsen calibration. Their headline practical result is that temperature scaling — a single scalar T>0 dividing the logits before softmax — is a simple post-hoc fix that reduces ECE without changing accuracy.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) ECE = sample-weighted average of |accuracy - confidence| over M equally-spaced confidence bins.
- (ev_002) The paper's reported ECE uses M = 15 bins.
- (ev_003) Reliability diagrams plot accuracy vs. confidence; the identity diagonal is perfect calibration and deviations are miscalibration.
- (ev_004) Temperature scaling: single scalar T>0 divides logits before softmax, optimized for NLL on a validation set.
- (ev_005) T does not change the argmax, so accuracy is unchanged while ECE drops.
- (ev_006) Modern nets are overconfident; depth, width, weight decay, and BatchNorm worsen calibration.

## Limitations
- Tertiary definitional anchor only. Empirical results are from 2017-era image/document classifiers (ResNet/DenseNet etc.) and do not directly address token-logprob calibration in modern generative LLMs; transfer of findings should be flagged as inference, not asserted from this source.
