# Source Card: Systematic Evaluation of Uncertainty Estimation Methods in Large Language Models

## Summary
This arXiv preprint (Oct 2025) systematically benchmarks four LLM uncertainty-estimation methods — verbalized confidence (VCE), maximum sequence probability (MSP), sample consistency, and the hybrid Confidence-Consistency Aggregation (CoCoA) — on BoolQ, SQuAD 2.0, TriviaQA, and GSM8K using a LLaMA 3.2 3B-Instruct model. It finds that single-shot verbalized confidence is systematically overconfident, that probability-based MSP is a strong ranker but only moderately calibrated, and that the hybrid CoCoA delivers the best overall reliability (lowest calibration error plus competitive-to-best discrimination). Multi-sample TOP-K aggregation substantially mitigates VCE overconfidence.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Four QA benchmarks (BoolQ, SQuAD 2.0, TriviaQA, GSM8K) evaluated on a LLaMA 3 / 3.2 3B-Instruct model across VCE, MSP, Sample Consistency, and CoCoA.
- (ev_002) Headline finding: the hybrid CoCoA approach yields the best overall reliability, improving both calibration and discrimination.
- (ev_003) Single-sample VCE is severely overconfident (~97.6% on SQuAD, 98.8% on GSM8K) despite lower accuracy, yielding large ECE.
- (ev_004) MSP beats single-shot VCE on calibration: GSM8K ECE 0.104 vs 0.335; TriviaQA ECE 0.147 vs 0.309.
- (ev_005) Multi-sample TOP-K aggregation cuts VCE ECE on GSM8K from 0.335 to 0.129.
- (ev_006) CoCoA is best-calibrated overall: ECE 0.062 on SQuAD 2.0 and 0.081 on GSM8K.
- (ev_007) MSP is a dependable ranker (highest AUROC on TriviaQA 0.841 / GSM8K 0.838) but with weaker calibration than the hybrid.
- (ev_008) CoCoA wins/ties discrimination: AUROC SQuAD 0.844, TriviaQA 0.841, GSM8K 0.786, BoolQ 0.687.

## Limitations
- Single small open model (LLaMA 3.2 3B-Instruct) on four QA datasets; authors note their focus is relative behavior of estimators and expect trends to transfer, but absolute numbers may shift across model sizes/families. CoCoA's strength comes at high compute cost (M=10 samples via separate decoding). Results table figures are rendered inline (digits doubled in scraped HTML, e.g. "0.3350.335"); confirmed against the canonical Table 1 values.
