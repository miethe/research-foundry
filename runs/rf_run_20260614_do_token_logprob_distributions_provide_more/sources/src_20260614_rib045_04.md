# Source Card: How to Fix a Broken Confidence Estimator: Evaluating Post-hoc Methods for Selective Classification with Deep Neural Networks

## Summary
This UAI 2024 paper (Cattelan & Silva) studies post-hoc confidence estimators for selective classification — methods that replace a model's confidence score without retraining — across 84 pretrained ImageNet classifiers. Its central result is that a simple p-norm normalization of the logits followed by taking the maximum logit repairs "broken" confidence estimators, after which selective-classification performance is almost entirely determined by accuracy. The fix is evaluated with the (Normalized) Area Under the Risk-Coverage curve and shown to remain consistent under distribution shift.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Extensive evaluation of post-hoc confidence estimators across 84 pretrained ImageNet classifiers, computed from logits with no retraining.
- (ev_002) A simple p-norm normalization of logits, then max logit, yields considerable selective-classification gains and fixes pathological "broken" confidence behavior.
- (ev_003) After the fix, selective-classification performance becomes almost entirely determined by accuracy.
- (ev_004) Results hold under distribution shift, indicating robustness to drift.
- (ev_005) AURC (area under the risk-coverage curve) is used as the selective-classification metric.
- (ev_006) A Normalized AURC (NAURC) is defined, mapping ideal to 0 and random to 1 for cross-classifier comparison.

## Limitations
- Experiments are confined to ImageNet image classifiers with softmax outputs; the paper does not directly test transfer of the logit-based fix to LLM token-logprob or selective-generation settings, so applicability to language-model vaults is an inference, not a demonstrated result.
