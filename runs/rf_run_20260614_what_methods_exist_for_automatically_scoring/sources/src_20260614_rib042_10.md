# Source Card: DeepResearch Bench: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Deep Research Agents

## Summary
DeepResearch Bench (arXiv:2506.11763) is a benchmark of 100 expert-crafted PhD-level research tasks across 22 fields, split evenly between English and Chinese, for evaluating LLM-based Deep Research Agents. It introduces two evaluation methodologies the authors report align strongly with human judgment: RACE, a reference-based report-quality scorer with adaptive, task-specific criteria across Comprehensiveness, Depth/Insight, Instruction-Following, and Readability; and FACT, which measures information-gathering quality via effective citation count and overall citation accuracy by verifying statement-citation pairs. The benchmark and key framework components are open-sourced.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) DeepResearch Bench = 100 PhD-level tasks crafted by domain experts across 22 fields.
- (ev_002) Authors propose two evaluation methodologies reported to align strongly with human judgment.
- (ev_003) RACE is a reference-based method with adaptive criteria for report quality.
- (ev_004) FACT assesses information retrieval via effective citation count and overall citation accuracy.
- (ev_005) RACE scores four dimensions (Comprehensiveness, Insight/Depth, Instruction-Following, Readability) with dynamic criteria and reference-based scoring.
- (ev_006) FACT extracts statement-URL pairs and verifies citation accuracy via scraping plus LLM judgment.
- (ev_007) Tasks split 50 English / 50 Chinese across 22 fields.

## Limitations
- arXiv preprint (not formally peer-reviewed).
