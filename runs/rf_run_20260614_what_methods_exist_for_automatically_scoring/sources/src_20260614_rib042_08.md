# Source Card: FActScore: Fine-grained Atomic Evaluation of Factual Precision in Long Form Text Generation

## Summary
FActScore (Min, Krishna et al., EMNLP 2023) introduces a fine-grained factuality metric that decomposes a long-form LM generation into atomic facts and reports the percentage supported by a reliable knowledge source. The paper validates the metric with extensive human evaluation of people-biography generations (ChatGPT scored only 58%) and introduces a retrieval-plus-LM automated estimator that tracks human scores within a less-than-2% error rate. The automated metric was applied to 6,500 generations from 13 recent LMs that would have cost roughly $26K to evaluate manually, finding GPT-4 and ChatGPT more factual than public models, with Vicuna and Alpaca among the best open-source.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) FActScore = percentage of a generation's atomic facts supported by a reliable knowledge source, after decomposition into atomic facts.
- (ev_002) Automated estimator (retrieval + strong LM) approximates human FActScore with less than a 2% error rate.
- (ev_003) Human-evaluated ChatGPT FActScore on people biographies was only 58%.
- (ev_004) Automated metric scored 6,500 generations from 13 LMs that would have cost about $26K to evaluate by humans.
- (ev_005) GPT-4 and ChatGPT beat public models; Vicuna and Alpaca were among the best open-source.
- (ev_006) The metric is motivated by mixed supported/unsupported content in long-form generations and the cost of human evaluation.

## Limitations
- Reported FActScores are scoped to people-biography generations and the specific LMs and knowledge source used; the less-than-2% error figure is the authors' own automated-vs-human comparison rather than an independent replication.
