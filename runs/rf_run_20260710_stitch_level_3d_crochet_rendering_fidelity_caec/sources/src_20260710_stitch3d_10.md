# Source Card: KnitWit design-spec.md (project seed, ChatGPT 5.2)

## Summary
This is the KnitWit project seed/design brief (authored by ChatGPT 5.2) that frames a "one-stop crochet OS" and decomposes its hardest, most novel feature — 3D visualization and reverse pattern generation — into a staged capability ladder (L0-L3). It argues for structural-geometry 3D rather than yarn-level physics, sketches a Crochet intermediate representation (IR) to enable playback and 3D mapping, and cites credible academic prior art (CrochetPARADE, a 2025 EG force-directed-layout paper, and an ACM amigurumi paper) while flagging reverse 3D-to-pattern synthesis as a Phase 3 endeavor full of "dragons."

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Four-level 3D capability ladder: L0 (2D + counters + highlighting) through L3 (3D object to pattern synthesis).
- (ev_002) Recommends structural geometry first (stitch graph/surface with thickness, textured to suggest yarn), not yarn physics.
- (ev_003) Sketches a Crochet IR: units, pieces[], rows[] of counted stitch ops, constraints/checkpoints, and assembly/placement markers.
- (ev_004) Cites CrochetPARADE, a 2025 EG force-directed-layout paper, and an ACM amigurumi paper as non-physics 3D paths.
- (ev_005) A transparent "ghost next row" overlay is called doable in 2D now, 3D later.
- (ev_006) Reverse 3D-to-pattern generation is Phase 3, needing watertight meshes/sane topology and accounting for per-user tension variance.
