# Source Card: CrochetBench: Can Vision-Language Models Move from Describing to Doing in Crochet Domain?

## Summary
CrochetBench is a benchmark from a University of Notre Dame group (Li et al., arXiv 2511.09483v2) that tests whether vision-language models can move beyond describing crochet to actually executing it, via four tasks: stitch recognition, instruction selection, instruction generation, and project-level translation into the executable CrochetPARADE DSL. Across nine open- and closed-source models (3B-72B), the study finds that surface-level fluency does not imply procedural correctness: models recognize stitches reasonably (Claude Sonnet 4 F1 60.94%) but collapse on executable program synthesis (5-8% for the best, most below 3%) and instruction generation (4.93% BLEU). It documents failure modes including irreversible early-step errors and uncontrolled "invented" undefined stitches, while showing image grounding helps with under-specified structural cues.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Even the strongest VLMs produce only 5-8% executable CrochetPARADE programs; most fall below 3%.
- (ev_002) Best instruction-generation system (Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite) reaches only 4.93% BLEU and 30.50% ChrF.
- (ev_003) Stitch recognition is strongest task; Claude Sonnet 4 leads with F1 60.94%.
- (ev_004) Instruction-selection accuracy clusters around 55-60% for most large/closed models.
- (ev_005) Early-step errors propagate irreversibly; correctness relies on continuation heuristics, not procedural understanding.
- (ev_006) Image grounding helps interpret repeated motifs, symmetry, shaping, and termination conditions under-specified in text.
- (ev_007) Larger models show higher undefined-stitch ("invented stitch") error rates, indicating uncontrolled symbolic invention.
- (ev_008) Nine models spanning ~3B-72B evaluated; Li et al., University of Notre Dame, arXiv 2511.09483v2.

## Limitations
- Preprint (arXiv v2), not yet formally peer-reviewed; results are for the specific CrochetPARADE DSL and the 2025 model lineup, so absolute scores will shift as models evolve. One key_point claim about "long-range stitch-count drift" and "unbalanced bracket syntax" as named failure modes could not be confirmed verbatim against the fetched HTML text (the source describes undefined-stitch/bracket DSL-control errors and early-step instability), so those specifics are recorded conservatively.
