# Source Card: Amigurumi Crochet Patterns from Geodesic Distances

## Summary
This Bridges 2024 short paper (pp. 369-372) by Mirela Ben Chen and Michal Edelstein (Technion) presents an inverse-amigurumi method that takes a 3D mesh plus a seed point and emits a human-readable crochet pattern. The core idea is to compute geodesic distance from the seed, treat equidistant curves as crochet rows, sample stitches non-uniformly under surface-curvature guidance, and use mean curvature at crease lines to pick stitch types (e.g., BLO/FLO). It is a compact, citable overview of the fuller AmiGo (SCF 2022) work, framing amigurumi shaping as a geodesic-distance plus curvature problem rather than a fixed-rule problem.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Computes geodesic distance from a seed point, then samples equidistant curves non-uniformly guided by surface curvature.
- (ev_002) Mean curvature at crease lines guides which stitch types are used to better adhere to the input shape.
- (ev_003) Constant-height rounds make each row's stitches equidistant from the seed, so geodesic distance best models crochet rows.
- (ev_004) Saddle-like (negative Gaussian and mean curvature) regions get adapted sampling with fewer stitches - curvature-bounded density.
- (ev_005) High-absolute-curvature regions orthogonal to crocheting direction trigger automatic BLO/FLO crease stitches.
- (ev_006) Explicitly references AmiGo (SCF 2022) as the fuller treatment and lineage for inverse amigurumi generation.
- (ev_007) Authors call for standardization of crochet instructions to enable tool interoperability.

## Limitations
- Short-paper overview; full algorithmic detail is deferred to the AmiGo SCF 2022 paper (ref [2]). The system joins-and-turns rather than crocheting in a spiral, so outputs do not look like "classic" amigurumi, and higher stitches / color / yarn estimation are listed as future work.
