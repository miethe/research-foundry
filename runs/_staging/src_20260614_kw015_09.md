# Source Card: ChartMuseum: Testing Visual Reasoning Capabilities of Large Vision-Language Models

## Summary
ChartMuseum is a 2025 chart question-answering benchmark (1,162 expert-annotated tuples from 928 real-world charts across 184 websites) built to separate textual from visual reasoning in large vision-language models. It shows a large, unsaturated gap between models and humans: humans hit 93% while the best proprietary model (Gemini-2.5-Pro) reaches only 63.0% and the best open-source model (Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct) only 38.5%. The benchmark isolates visual reasoning as the dominant failure mode, where all models drop 35%-55% versus text-heavy questions while humans stay near-perfect (98.2%).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) ChartMuseum is a chart-QA benchmark of 1,162 expert-annotated image-question-answer tuples drawn from 928 unique real-world charts across 184 websites.
- (ev_002) Humans reach 93% accuracy while the best proprietary model Gemini-2.5-Pro attains only 63.0% and the leading open-source LVLM Qwen2.5-VL-72B-Instruct reaches only 38.5%.
- (ev_003) There is a 24.5-point accuracy gap between the best open-source model (38.5%) and the best proprietary model (63.0%).
- (ev_004) On primarily-visual-reasoning questions, all models drop 35%-55% relative to text-reasoning-heavy questions.
- (ev_005) Humans remain near-perfect on the sampled visual-reasoning set (56/57, 98.2%).
- (ev_006) Unlike prior chart benchmarks near saturation, ChartMuseum exposes a substantial model-vs-human gap and differentiates model capabilities.
- (ev_007) The hardest visual skill category is visual comparison (comparing objects/groups by size, height, or spatial placement).

## Limitations
- Benchmark numbers (dataset construction and model scores) are self-reported by the authors of an arXiv preprint and depend on the paper's prompting and evaluation harness; scores reflect models available as of the May 2025 (v3) snapshot and may not generalize to later model versions.
