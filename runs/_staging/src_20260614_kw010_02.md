# Source Card: Trusting AI: does uncertainty visualization affect decision-making?

## Summary
This peer-reviewed empirical study (Frontiers in Computer Science, Feb 2025, n=147) tests whether visualizing an AI agent's prediction uncertainty changes user trust, confidence, and decisions. It finds that uncertainty visualization increased trust for nearly half of participants, with an especially strong effect among those holding negative attitudes toward AI, and that the visual encoding choice (size, transparency, color saturation) materially affects perceived trust and intuitiveness. The authors recommend tailoring uncertainty representation to baseline attitude while explicitly cautioning against engineering over-trust or over-reliance.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Uncertainty visualization significantly enhanced trust for 58% of participants with negative AI attitudes.
- (ev_002) Trust in AI increased for 48% (n=70) of the full 147-participant sample.
- (ev_003) Size-based uncertainty encoding had a significantly greater trust impact than transparency (p < 0.05).
- (ev_004) Color saturation was rated highest for intuition and preference, above transparency and size.
- (ev_005) Decision confidence rose for 44% but fell for 56% of participants when uncertainty was shown.
- (ev_006) 33% (n=48) of participants changed their responses after seeing the AI's uncertainty.
- (ev_007) Authors decline to endorse any single technique and caution against pushing users to over-trust AI.

## Limitations
- Authors explicitly decline to endorse a specific visualization technique; effects are attitude-dependent and split (e.g., confidence rose for some, fell for more).
- Online/crowdsourced participant sample; trust and decision-change effects vary by individual baseline attitude toward AI.
