# Source Card: Ravelry: Terms of Service

## Summary
Ravelry's Terms of Service (last updated February 1, 2017) define the contractual framework for pattern listing, licensing, and copyright on the platform. Designers retain copyright in their patterns and warrant they hold the necessary rights; posting grants Ravelry only a non-exclusive, revocable license to list and advertise the work. Sellers must grant buyers at least a non-commercial, perpetual personal-use license, users may not create derivative works from patterns they do not own, and copyright complaints follow a DMCA-style process directed to legal@ravelry.com.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Users warrant they hold the copyright and necessary IP rights in every pattern they list.
- (ev_002) Posting grants Ravelry only a non-exclusive, revocable license to list and advertise the pattern.
- (ev_003) Users may not modify or create derivative works from patterns they do not own absent an explicit separate license.
- (ev_004) Sellers must grant buyers at minimum a non-commercial, perpetual personal-use license.
- (ev_005) Collecting or storing personal data about other users is prohibited except as explicitly permitted.
- (ev_006) Copyright complaints go to the Copyright Agent at legal@ravelry.com under a DMCA-style process.

## Limitations
