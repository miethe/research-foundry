# Source Card: Directive (EU) 2019/790 on Copyright in the Digital Single Market (Articles 3 & 4, Recital 18)

## Summary
Directive (EU) 2019/790 establishes two text-and-data-mining (TDM) copyright exceptions in EU law. Article 4 provides a general TDM exception for reproductions and extractions of lawfully accessible works, but only where rightholders have not expressly reserved the use - and for content made publicly available online, that reservation must be by machine-readable means (Recital 18). Article 3 provides a separate, non-overridable mandatory TDM exception for research organisations and cultural heritage institutions with lawful access. Both exceptions require lawful access, and Member States were to transpose the directive by 7 June 2021.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The general Article 4 TDM exception covers reproductions/extractions of lawfully accessible works and subject matter.
- (ev_002) Article 4 copies may be retained only as long as necessary for TDM purposes.
- (ev_003) The Article 4 exception applies only if use has not been expressly reserved in an appropriate (machine-readable, online) manner.
- (ev_004) Recital 18: online publicly-available content rights are reserved appropriately only via machine-readable means (metadata, site terms).
- (ev_005) Article 3 grants a separate, non-overridable scientific-research TDM exception for research orgs and cultural heritage institutions with lawful access (Article 4(4): Article 4 "shall not affect" Article 3).
- (ev_006) Transposition deadline was 7 June 2021; lawful access is a precondition for both exceptions.

## Limitations
- The directive sets a minimum standard; enforceable rights depend on each EU Member State's national transposition, which can vary in detail.
- Quoted text is the directive itself, not implementing national legislation or case law interpreting it.
