# Source Card: Parsing Semi-structured Languages: A Crochet Pattern to Diagram Translation

## Summary
This peer-reviewed SAICSIT 2023 conference paper (Springer CCIS vol. 1878) by Lisa van Staden and Lynette van Zijl establishes that conventionally formatted crochet patterns are a semi-structured natural language restrictive enough to be parsed with classic multi-phase compiler methods, avoiding general NLP. It implements a two-parser pipeline: the first parser maps patterns into a uniform formal-language representation applicable to most patterns, and the second transpiles that formal language into a crochet diagram. The authors report success across a wide range of patterns, supporting a formal-grammar import lane for structured text patterns while implicitly bounding tractability to the conventional/structured subset.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Crochet patterns are restrictive enough for standard multi-phase compiler parsing instead of NLP.
- (ev_002) Two-parser pipeline: pattern to uniform formal-language representation, then formal language to diagram.
- (ev_003) Reported successful for a wide range of different patterns.
- (ev_004) Formal-language representation applies to "most" patterns, implicitly bounding tractability to a structured subset.
- (ev_005) Approach grounded in classic compiler phases (lexical, syntactic, semantic analysis, code generation).
- (ev_006) Translation framed as a transpiler; references include the ANTLR parser generator, indicating conventional grammar tooling.
- (ev_007) Parser must normalize crochet-dialect variation (American vs. British stitch terminology).

## Limitations
- Full text is paywalled; evidence is drawn from the publisher abstract, notes, and reference list rather than the methods/results sections, so quantitative success metrics and the exact pattern-coverage scope are not captured here.
- The originating brief mislabeled the venue as ICDAR-affiliated Springer LNCS; the paper actually appears in Springer CCIS vol. 1878 (SAICSIT 2023). Tractability claims apply to the restricted/conventional pattern subset ("most patterns"), not arbitrary freeform prose, charts, or scans.
