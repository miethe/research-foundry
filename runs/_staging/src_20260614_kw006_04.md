# Source Card: Amigurumi Crochet Patterns from Geodesic Distances

## Summary
This Bridges 2024 short paper (Ben Chen & Edelstein, Technion; pp. 369-372) gives a concise overview of generating an amigurumi crochet pattern from an input 3D model by computing geodesic distance from a seed point and treating the equidistant isoline curves as crochet rows. Surface curvature non-uniformly guides row sampling (fewer stitches in saddle regions, inflation to remove non-realizable "craters"), and mean curvature at crease lines selects stitch types (BLO/FLO) so the crocheted skin better adheres to the input shape. It is positioned as a brief introduction to the authors' fuller AmiGo method (SCF 2022).

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Core method: geodesic distance from a seed, non-uniform curvature-guided sampling of equidistant curves, mean-curvature-driven stitch-type choice at creases.
- (ev_002) Positioned as a brief introduction to the fuller "AmiGo" SCF publication; figures reproduced with permission.
- (ev_003) Crochet-in-the-round with constant SC row height makes geodesic distance the natural model for rows.
- (ev_004) Equidistant curves spaced by stitch height define rows; stitch locations via flattening + even sampling -> crochet graph.
- (ev_005) Saddle regions reduce stitch counts; creases use BLO/FLO where curvature is high and orthogonal to crocheting direction; both automatic.
- (ev_006) "Crater" regions (negative mean / positive Gaussian curvature) are non-realizable and are removed by inflating the input.
- (ev_007) Multi-component equidistant curves trigger automatic partitioning into separately-crochetable parts; sewing-free, seamless assembly.
- (ev_008) Results differ from classic amigurumi (join-and-turn vs spiral); spiral support and other features planned.

## Limitations
- Short paper (4 pages) presenting an overview only; the full method/details live in the AmiGo SCF 2022 publication.
- No code/software link or formal DOI is provided in this PDF; available via the Bridges Archive PDF only.
- Authors acknowledge output does not look like "classic" amigurumi due to the join-and-turn construction.
