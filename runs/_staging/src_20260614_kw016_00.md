# Source Card: CrochetBench: Can Vision-Language Models Move from Describing to Doing in Crochet Domain?

## Summary
CrochetBench is a first-party arXiv benchmark (Li et al., 2025) of 6,085 Yarnspirations crochet patterns across 55 categories, normalized via a GPT-4o-mini pipeline and evaluated with the CrochetPARADE DSL so correctness is checked by execution rather than text similarity. Across four tasks of increasing difficulty, nine VLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite, Claude Sonnet 4, and open models) show a sharp collapse from recognition (best F1 60.94%) to synthesis, with even the strongest models producing only 5-8% executable programs. The work establishes that fluent crochet-like text is decoupled from structural validity, and that neither scaling nor supervised finetuning closes the recognition-to-synthesis gap.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) 6,085 crochet patterns across 55 distinct project categories.
- (ev_002) Sourced from Yarnspirations PDFs, normalized via a GPT-4o-mini conversion pipeline.
- (ev_003) Uses the CrochetPARADE DSL for structural validation and execution-based evaluation.
- (ev_004) Nine VLMs evaluated, including GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite, Claude Sonnet 4, and 3B-72B open models.
- (ev_005) Best stitch-recognition F1 is 60.94% (Claude Sonnet 4); instruction selection plateaus at 55-60%.
- (ev_006) Sharp drop from recognition/retrieval to synthesis of coherent multi-step procedures.
- (ev_007) Even the strongest models produce only 5-8% executable programs at the project level.
- (ev_008) Fluent crochet-like text is decoupled from structural validity; scaling/finetuning do not close the gap.

## Limitations
- arXiv preprint (v1 Nov 2025, v2 Feb 2026), not formally peer-reviewed. Some statistics from the supplied brief (specific bracket/reference syntax error percentages and a ~21% Qwen2-VL project-level valid rate) appear in figures/appendix and could not be verified as short verbatim text in the source; they were omitted rather than invented. The verbatim best-model project-level executable rate is the 5-8% range.
