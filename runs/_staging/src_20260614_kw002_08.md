# Source Card: CrochetPARADE Manual (Crochet PAttern Renderer, Analyzer, and DEbugger)

## Summary
The CrochetPARADE Manual is the primary, author-written documentation for a crochet pattern domain-specific language and its renderer/analyzer/debugger toolchain. It defines a compact textual grammar for stitches (built-in tokens, post/loop variants, increase/decrease suffixes), repeats and row/round control, worked-into attachment operators, and an extensible DEF mechanism with a raw node/connection grammar. A force-directed physics engine converges stitch geometry (reported to within ~10% of requested lengths) and exports SVG charts plus GLTF/OBJ/STL 3D models. Licensing is split: GPLv3 code, public-domain grammar/language, and CC BY-NC-SA manual.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) CrochetPARADE expands to "Crochet PAttern Renderer, Analyzer, and DEbugger", framing it as a crochet DSL with tooling.
- (ev_002) Increases/decreases are stitch-token suffixes (Ninc/Ntog); sc2inc = two single crochets into the same stitch.
- (ev_003) Bracketed blocks with integer multipliers express repeats; [10sc,turn]*3 expands to three identical rows.
- (ev_004) Custom stitches via DEF use a raw grammar: top_nodes:bottom_nodes~attachments:other_nodes:connections.
- (ev_005) The grammar/language is explicitly public domain and not copyrightable.
- (ev_006) Code/computational components are GPLv3; the manual is CC BY-NC-SA.
- (ev_007) The physics engine is approximate: author reports convergence to within ~10% of requested stitch lengths on default settings.

## Limitations
- The ~10% convergence figure is the author's self-reported observation ("In most experiments I have run...") rather than an independently benchmarked result; treat the precision claim as approximate and engine/setting dependent.
- Source is single-author primary documentation; it describes the tool's intended behavior, not external validation of correctness or adoption.
