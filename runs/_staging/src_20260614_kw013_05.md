# Source Card: KnitWit Design Spec (project seed, ChatGPT 5.2)

## Summary
This is the KnitWit project seed/design spec authored via ChatGPT 5.2 (created Feb 2026, updated Apr 2026). It establishes the app's scope as a crochet/amigurumi "OS" combining a pattern library, row-by-row playback, project management, and 3D visualization. Most relevant to the copyright/contract question, it explicitly names "importing patterns from anywhere" as the #1 business landmine and recommends designer opt-in, licensing, or user-private imports as the mitigation path — framing pattern ingestion (parse into a structured Crochet IR) as the legally sensitive activity.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The spec self-identifies importing patterns from anywhere as the app's single biggest business risk.
- (ev_002) The spec assumes IP/licensing must be handled carefully because many patterns aren't legally scrape-and-import friendly.
- (ev_003) The product requires a structured Crochet IR (units, pieces, rows, constraints, assembly) — parsing/storing third-party pattern text.
- (ev_004) Pattern import competes with My Row Counter, which imports PDFs/webpages/videos and integrates with Ravelry.
- (ev_005) Ribblr is cited as the incumbent positioning interactive ePatterns as its innovation.
- (ev_006) The spec recommends an amigurumi-first scope as the differentiating wedge.

## Limitations
- AI-authored project seed (ChatGPT 5.2) with no legal review; its IP/licensing framing is product-strategy intuition, not a legal authority. Competitor and market claims are cited to ChatGPT-tagged web links that are not independently verified in this card.
