# Source Card: Immortalis-G925: The Fragment Prepass

## Summary
Arm's official Mobile, Graphics, and Gaming developer blog post (by Tord Oygard, Nov 2024) explains the Fragment Pre-pass, a Hidden Surface Removal technique in Arm's 2024-2025 GPUs (Mali-G625, Mali-G725, Immortalis-G925) that determines which fragments will be visible before shading them. It reports vendor-measured efficiency gains on real games and notes that the technique extends HSR to a useful subset of transparent draw calls beyond what a typical hardware prepass handles, with Forward Pixel Kill providing fallback HSR.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) The Fragment Pre-pass is an HSR technique running a first pass to find which fragments will be visible.
- (ev_002) Fortnite parachute scene: 6.5% fewer GPU cycles and 16.1% fewer floating-point operations.
- (ev_003) Justice Online Mobile: 4.6% fewer GPU cycles and 15.9% fewer floating-point operations.
- (ev_004) HSR extends to "a useful subset of transparent draw calls" where a hardware prepass would normally give up.
- (ev_005) Forward Pixel Kill remained on as fallback HSR during testing.
- (ev_006) Supported on Mali-G625, Mali-G725, and Immortalis-G925.

## Limitations
- Vendor-published performance figures from Arm's own blog; benchmark scenes are vendor-selected and not independently verified. Page metadata lists the publish date as 2024-11-28, which differs from the harness-supplied "2025-05" hint.
