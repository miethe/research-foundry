# Source Card: What does it cost to sell my patterns on Ribblr? — Ribblr Help Center

## Summary
Ribblr's first-party Help Center states that opening a shop and listing patterns is free — there are no listing or shop fees, and a designer pays only a single fee when a sale occurs. That sale fee can be reduced by up to 100% (i.e., eliminated) through SelFee, a points-based "smart fee" earned by sharing personal shop and pattern links. The exact base sale-fee percentage and any per-sale minimum floor are not stated on the Ribblr pages accessed; only the qualitative no-fixed-fee / single-sale-fee / up-to-100%-off structure is confirmed verbatim.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Opening a Ribblr shop is free; no listing or shop fees; a single fee only on a sale.
- (ev_002) For-designers page reiterates zero listing/shop/Ribbuild fees and a single sale fee removable via SelFee.
- (ev_003) SelFee is a personal "smart fee" that can reduce the sale fee by up to 100%.
- (ev_004) SelFee discounts come from a points system tied to sharing links (e.g., 10,000 points = 10% off).
- (ev_005) Ribblr frames SelFee as giving designers full control over fees on its interactive selling format.

## Limitations
- The specific "4% per sale" rate and "25 cents minimum" floor referenced in the launch brief were NOT found in the text of the Ribblr Help Center page, the Ribblr-for-designers page, or the SelFee help page as accessed on 2026-06-14; those numbers are therefore omitted to avoid fabrication. The card establishes the qualitative fee structure (free listing/shop, single sale fee, up-to-100%-off via SelFee) only.
- No publish date is visible on the pages; "2025" is carried from the brief and should be treated as approximate.
