# Source Card: Representing Crochet with Stitch Meshes

## Summary
A peer-reviewed SCF '20 paper (Guo, Lin, Narayanan, McCann; Carnegie Mellon) showing that crochet patterns can be represented within the "stitch mesh" paradigm: a library of edge-labelled tiles, each holding explicit yarn geometry, that connect along their edges. The key adaptation is a "current loop" edge type capturing the loop held on the hook, plus short-term (Next/Previous) and long-term (Future/Past) edge labels that constrain how stitch faces tile a surface. The authors model only a subset of stitches plus utility faces and generate patterns from 3D models via the Narayanan et al. 2019 remeshing pipeline, while documenting fidelity failures at the render/relaxation step.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Crochet is represented as a stitch mesh: a tile library where each tile holds yarn geometry and tiles connect along edges.
- (ev_002) A special "current loop" edge type captures the loop of yarn held on the crochet hook.
- (ev_003) Four edge labels (Next/Previous short-term, Future/Past long-term) connect as previous-next and past-future pairs to tile a surface.
- (ev_004) Faces use short-term (orange) and long-term (purple) edges; leading loop and free yarn cross short-term edges, only loops cross long-term edges.
- (ev_005) Only chain, slip, single crochet, sc2tog, and sc3tog are modeled; the tile set is "far from complete" and double/treble crochet are future work.
- (ev_006) The Narayanan et al. 2019 pipeline maps quad faces to sc/turn, pentagons to sc2tog, and increase triangles to inc, demonstrated on cube, sphere, and Stanford bunny.
- (ev_007) The Stanford bunny body became more "lacy" with larger holes than the ears due to non-uniform stitch density from remeshing.
- (ev_008) The ad-hoc yarn-relaxation/simulation approach produced dense/gappy artifacts, with small holes and non-ideal shaping from stitch-mesh generation.

## Limitations
- Scope: only a subset of crochet stitches (chain, slip, single crochet, sc2tog, sc3tog) plus utility faces (inc, turn, cap, ch_edge) were modeled; double/treble crochet, post stitches, and many variations are unmodeled. The yarn-relaxation/simulation step is explicitly described as ad-hoc and produces shape-instability and fidelity artifacts; automatic pattern generation relies on a single stitch aspect ratio. 2020 work; no quantitative accuracy metrics reported.
