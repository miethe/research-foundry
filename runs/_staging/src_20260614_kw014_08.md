# Source Card: KnitWit Design Spec (ChatGPT 5.2) — Monetization section

## Summary
This ChatGPT 5.2-authored design spec for the KnitWit crochet app proposes a three-part monetization model (marketplace cut on pattern sales, a pro subscription tier, and add-on packs) and positions the product as "Ravelry + interactive pattern runner + CAD-lite for amigurumi." It identifies the 3D visualizer and reverse-pattern-from-3D as the novel competitive wedge against incumbents Ribblr and My Row Counter, and flags pattern IP/copyright as the top business risk, recommending designer opt-in / licensing.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Three monetization mechanics proposed: marketplace cut + designer storefronts, pro subscription tier, and add-on packs.
- (ev_002) Subscription tier gates 3D preview, advanced import/parsing, and pattern generation as "pro" features.
- (ev_003) Product positioned as "Ravelry + interactive pattern runner + CAD-lite for amigurumi."
- (ev_004) Pattern IP/copyright from broad importing flagged as the #1 business landmine; designer opt-in / licensing recommended.
- (ev_005) Ribblr and My Row Counter named as dominant "pattern + tracking" competitors creating a red ocean for basic features.
- (ev_006) 3D-to-pattern generation recommended as a Phase 3 capability, after playback and structural 3D preview.
- (ev_007) 3D visualizer + reverse-pattern-from-3D identified as the novel, defensible product wedge.
