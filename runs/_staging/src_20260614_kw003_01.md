# Source Card: Translation of User Crochet Patterns to CrochetPARADE Syntax Using Large Language Models

## Summary
A 2025 AAAI Summer Symposium Series paper (Dias & Karim, Heriot-Watt University) that builds the first structured, open-source dataset pairing user-generated crochet patterns with CrochetPARADE DSL translations, then evaluates baseline, few-shot, and fine-tuning LLM approaches for prose-to-DSL translation. The best configuration (fine-tuned DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama8b) reached only 74% accuracy, establishing that general prose-to-DSL crochet translation remains unreliable even when a model is dedicated and fine-tuned for the task.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Authored by Dias & Karim (Heriot-Watt University); published 2025-08-01 in AAAI Symposium Series Vol. 6 No. 1, pp. 200-208.
- (ev_002) CrochetPARADE syntax differs from standard notation and may be non-intuitive, motivating automated translation as an accessibility layer.
- (ev_003) The study produced the first structured, open-source crochet-pattern dataset paired with CrochetPARADE translations for ML use.
- (ev_004) Baseline, few-shot, and fine-tuning approaches were evaluated across LLMs.
- (ev_005) Best result: fine-tuned DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama8b reached 74% accuracy (~1 in 4 patterns still wrong).

## Limitations
- The 74% best-case accuracy is taken from the abstract's headline metric; per-approach and per-model breakdowns (and the dataset size) live in the full PDF, not captured here. The "requires human verification" framing is an interpretation supported by the 74% ceiling rather than an explicit quote from the abstract.
