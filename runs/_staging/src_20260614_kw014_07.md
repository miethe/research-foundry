# Source Card: How does Ravelry make money?

## Summary
A first-party Ravelry blog post (2012) in which the company explains its revenue model: a lean, profitable four-person operation funded by a low/capped pattern-sales commission (free up to $20/month by volume), a large self-served advertising business (~180M page views/month, ~1,500 advertisers), and diversified streams including Amazon book affiliate commissions (7-8%), a $5/year forum-image "Extra," branded merchandise, and voluntary donations. It establishes that Ravelry deliberately avoids third-party ad fees by running its own ad-serving system.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Ravelry runs as a profitable four-person company.
- (ev_002) Pattern-sales commission scales from free up to a $20/month cap by sales volume.
- (ev_003) Over 1.3 million patterns sold since inception; a good income source.
- (ev_004) Custom in-house ad server avoids third-party fees/commissions.
- (ev_005) ~180M monthly page views and ~1,500 active advertisers; half spend under $15/month.
- (ev_006) Amazon book affiliate commissions of 7-8%.
- (ev_007) $5/year "Ravelry Extra" for forum image uploads.
- (ev_008) Diversified income from merchandise and voluntary donations.

## Limitations
- Self-reported, unaudited figures from a 2012 post; the patterns-sold count, page views, advertiser counts, and pricing are likely stale.
- The commonly cited ~3.5% commission applied between ~$30 and ~$1,500 in monthly sales appears in secondary reporting, NOT in this primary post, which only frames the commission as "free up to $20/month."
