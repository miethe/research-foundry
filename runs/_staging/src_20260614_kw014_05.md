# Source Card: State of Subscription Apps 2025

## Summary
RevenueCat's "State of Subscription Apps 2025" is a first-party benchmark report drawing on subscription performance data from roughly 75,000 apps and over $10B in tracked revenue across iOS and Android. The online preview reports conversion benchmarks across the funnel — download-to-trial, trial-to-paid, and download-to-paid — segmented by access method (freemium vs hard paywall), trial duration, price point, category, and geography. It establishes that hard paywalls and higher price points materially raise download-to-paid conversion, while longer trials (17-32 days) maximize trial-to-paid conversion.

## Key evidence
- (ev_001) Hard-paywall apps convert downloads to paid at a 12.11% median versus just 2.18% for freemium.
- (ev_002) Trials lasting 17-32 days have the highest median trial-to-paid conversion at 45.7%.
- (ev_003) Median download-to-trial conversion is 6.2%; top-decile (P90) apps reach 20.3%.
- (ev_004) High-priced apps convert download-to-trial at a 9.8% median versus 4.3% for low-priced apps.
- (ev_005) Benchmarks span ~75,000 subscription apps and $10B+ in tracked revenue.
- (ev_006) Higher-priced apps see a 2.7% median Day 35 download-to-paid rate versus 1.5% for low-priced apps.

## Limitations
- The online page is only a preview/subset of the full 263-page PDF report.
- Data reflects only apps on RevenueCat's platform, not the entire subscription-app market.
- This is a vendor-published report, so framing favors subscription monetization strategies; several precise figures appear in expert-commentary callouts rather than primary chart captions.
